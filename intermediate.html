Ukrainian President Zelensky plans to urge NATO for more air defence systems, including interceptor missiles, during a meeting in Turkey following recent intense Russian attacks.

Intermediate ESL: International Military Aid and Ukraine's Defence Needs

I. The News Brief

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky is preparing to press NATO leaders for increased military assistance. His main focus will be securing additional air defence systems for his country. This urgent request comes after a period of intense Russian strikes across Ukraine. These attacks have highlighted Ukraine's critical need for better protection against aerial threats. Zelensky specifically aims to receive more interceptor missiles to counter incoming projectiles. He plans to argue his case strongly during an upcoming NATO meeting held in Turkey. The President believes these systems are vital for safeguarding Ukrainian cities and infrastructure. The ongoing conflict continues to demonstrate the importance of robust aerial protection. International support in this area is crucial for Ukraine's ability to defend itself effectively. Therefore, the outcome of this meeting could significantly impact the course of the war.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. press (verb): To strongly urge someone to do something or to make a demand forcefully, often to persuade them.
2. air defence systems (noun phrase): Military equipment and strategies used to protect against attacks from aircraft or missiles, often designed to detect and destroy threats.
3. intense (adjective): Very strong, extreme, or severe; describing feelings, efforts, or actions that are felt or done to a high degree.
4. interceptor missiles (noun phrase): Guided missiles specifically designed to detect and destroy incoming enemy missiles or aircraft while they are in flight.
5. argue (verb): To give reasons or evidence in support of an idea, action, or theory, often with the aim of persuading others.

Grammar Focus: Reduced Relative Clauses
Reduced relative clauses help make sentences more concise by removing the relative pronoun (like 'which', 'who', 'that') and the verb 'to be' when they are followed by a participle (present or past). They provide essential or non-essential information about a noun.
Example from the text: "He plans to argue his case strongly during an upcoming NATO meeting held in Turkey."

Intermediate ESL: Ukraine-Russia conflict and military defense

I. The News Brief

Ukraine has issued a serious warning about a potential interceptor missile shortage following a large-scale attack. On Sunday, the capital city of Kyiv experienced what President Zelensky described as a "massive Russian attack." This onslaught involved a significant number of aerial weapons, specifically 68 missiles and 351 strike drones. The Ukrainian President's statement emphasized the sheer scale and intensity of the recent aggression. Such widespread attacks place immense strain on Ukraine's air defense capabilities and existing stockpiles. The immediate concern is the depletion of crucial defensive munitions needed to counter future threats. Ukrainian officials are urgently trying to address the declining reserves to maintain effective protection for their cities. The warning highlights the critical need for continued international support to supply more air defense systems. Nineteen people were reportedly killed in the Kyiv region as a direct result of this attack. This incident underscores the ongoing challenges Ukraine faces in protecting its civilian population and infrastructure.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. interceptor missile shortage (noun phrase): A situation where a country does not have enough missiles designed to stop enemy attacks in the air, creating a lack of vital defensive weapons.
2. massive (adjective): Something extremely large in size, amount, or degree; very big or powerful.
3. strike drones (noun phrase): Unmanned aerial vehicles that are designed to attack targets, often equipped with bombs or missiles.
4. onslaught (noun): A fierce or destructive attack, often sudden and overwhelming, usually involving many forces or actions.
5. stockpiles (noun): Large accumulated reserves of something, especially supplies or weapons, kept for future use.

Grammar Focus: Reporting Verbs
Reporting verbs are used when we tell someone what another person said, thought, or felt. Instead of using direct speech (quotation marks), we often use verbs like 'say', 'tell', 'warn', 'describe', 'emphasize', or 'report' followed by a 'that'-clause (which can sometimes be omitted) or an infinitive/gerund. The tense in the reported clause often shifts backwards.
Example from the text: "President Zelensky described as a "massive Russian attack.""

It is often used in news to report events where the agent is unknown, unimportant, or the focus is on the result.
Example from the text: "Mojtaba Khamenei, who is a prominent figure in Iran, has not been seen publicly for some time."

France recently experienced a severe heatwave resulting in thousands of excess deaths, while forecasters warn that Europe should prepare for even higher temperatures in the near future.

Intermediate ESL: Extreme Weather and Climate Change in Europe

I. The News Brief

France recently experienced a severe heatwave, leading to a significant rise in fatalities. Specifically, 2,025 excess deaths were recorded in the country during the peak of this extreme weather event. These figures represent more deaths than typically expected for that period, directly linked to the dangerously high temperatures. Meanwhile, forecasters are issuing urgent warnings across the continent about the developing weather patterns. Europe must now brace for even more extreme temperatures in the coming days, according to meteorological predictions. Weather models indicate that several countries will see temperatures soaring, potentially breaking new records. This ongoing pattern of severe heat raises serious concerns about public health and essential infrastructure. Authorities are advising citizens to take precautions, such as staying hydrated and avoiding direct sun exposure during the hottest parts of the day. Such prolonged periods of intense heat are becoming increasingly common due to the broader effects of climate change. The situation highlights the critical need for effective strategies to manage the impacts of future heatwaves.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. heatwave (noun): A period of unusually hot weather that lasts for several days or longer, often causing discomfort or danger.
2. excess deaths (noun phrase): The number of deaths above what would normally be expected for a given period, often linked to a specific event like a public health crisis or extreme weather.
3. forecasters (noun): People whose job is to predict future events or conditions, especially related to the weather.
4. brace for (phrasal verb): To prepare yourself for something difficult or unpleasant that is going to happen.
5. extreme temperatures (noun phrase): Temperatures that are much higher or lower than average, often reaching dangerous levels.

It's common in news reports when the 'doer' of the action is unknown, unimportant, or obvious, or when we want to emphasize the result.
Example from the text: "Specifically, 2,025 excess deaths were recorded in the country during the peak of this extreme weather event."
