Advanced Grammar: Reduced Relative Clauses
Reduced relative clauses are a sophisticated grammatical structure where the relative pronoun (e.g., who, which, that) and sometimes the auxiliary verb (e.g., is, are, was, were) are omitted, making the sentence more concise and formal. The clause often begins with a present participle (-ing form), a past participle (-ed form), or sometimes just an adjective or noun phrase. They provide additional information about a noun without using a full relative clause, characteristic of advanced English writing.
Example from the text: "The sheer scale of public attendance underscored the deep veneration many held for the departed figure."
