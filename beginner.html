📅 July 09, 2026 [0 days, 15 hours old] - US and Iran Trade Attacks: News for Learners
The United States and Iran recently exchanged military attacks.

Beginner ESL: International Conflict and Military Actions

I. The News Brief

The United States and Iran are in conflict. They recently trade attacks. This happened while Khamenei was buried. Centcom is a US military group. Centcom says it hit 90 Iranian targets. These were the latest strikes. The Iranian health ministry spoke. It says 14 people were killed. The deaths happened since Tuesday. This situation is very serious.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. trade attacks (verb phrase): To attack each other.
2. buried (verb (past participle)): Put a dead person in the ground.
3. hit (verb (past simple)): To strike something with force.
4. targets (noun): Places or things chosen to be attacked.
5. latest (adjective): The newest or most recent one.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense for Finished Actions
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that finished at a specific time in the past. Many verbs add -ed, but some common verbs are irregular and change their form completely.
Example from the text: "Centcom says it hit 90 Iranian targets."

📅 July 07, 2026 [2 days, 12 hours old] - Zelensky Asks Nato for More Air Defence

Beginner ESL: Zelensky's Request for Air Defence

President Zelensky is from Ukraine. He wants to speak with Nato. Nato is a group of countries. Zelensky will go to a meeting in Turkey. He will ask for help there. Ukraine needs more air defence systems. Russia made intense strikes on Ukraine. These strikes used missiles. Ukraine needs interceptor missiles. These missiles can stop Russian attacks.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

2. ask for (verb phrase): To say you want or need something from another person or group.
3. air defence systems (noun phrase): Special machines or weapons that protect a place from attacks coming from the sky, like planes or missiles.
4. intense strikes (noun phrase): Very strong and heavy attacks, often with many bombs or missiles.
5. interceptor missiles (noun phrase): Special rockets that are launched to hit and destroy other enemy rockets or aircraft in the air.

Grammar Focus: Future with 'will'
We use 'will' to talk about things that we plan to do or expect to happen in the future. It helps us speak about actions or decisions that have not happened yet.
Example from the text: "Zelensky will go to a meeting in Turkey."

📅 July 03, 2026 [6 days, 13 hours old] - Europe Faces Extreme Heat
France had many deaths because of a very hot period, and Europe expects more hot weather soon.

Beginner ESL: Extreme Weather and Heatwaves

I. The News Brief

France had a very hot time. This hot time was called a heatwave. More than 2,000 extra people died in France then. These were excess deaths because of the heat. This happened at the peak of the heatwave. Now, Europe is ready for more hot weather. Weather experts are called forecasters. Forecasters warn about extreme temperatures. These temperatures will come in the next few days. The continent of Europe will feel this hot weather.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. heatwave (noun): A time when the weather is much hotter than usual.
2. excess deaths (noun phrase): More people died than normal at that time.
3. peak (noun): The highest or strongest point of something.
4. forecasters (noun): People who say what the weather will be like in the future.
5. extreme temperatures (noun phrase): Weather that is much hotter or colder than normal.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the Simple Past tense to talk about actions that finished in the past. It tells us about things that already happened. We often add '-ed' to regular verbs or use special forms for irregular verbs.
Example from the text: "More than 2,000 extra people died in France then."
