📅 May 09, 2026 - Putin's Victory Day Speech
This lesson explores a news brief about President Putin's Victory Day speech, focusing on vocabulary, the passive voice, and comprehension.

Intermediate ESL: News Analysis

I. The News Brief

Russian President Vladimir Putin delivered a speech at Moscow's annual Victory Day parade. This important event commemorates the Soviet Union's victory over Nazi Germany in World War II. However, this year's parade was noticeably scaled back compared to previous celebrations. In his address, Putin strongly criticized NATO, accusing the alliance of posing a threat to Russia. He used the platform to present his justifications for the ongoing conflict in Ukraine. He referred to the conflict as a "special military operation," a term he has consistently used. Putin argued that Russia was forced to act to protect its security interests. The speech aimed to reinforce public support for his actions and condemn Western policies.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. denounces (verb): To strongly criticize someone or something in public.
2. scaled-back (adjective): Made smaller, simpler, or less impressive than before.
3. justify (verb): To give a good reason for something you have done, especially something that others might question.
4. special military operation (phrase): A term used by Russia to describe its invasion of Ukraine, avoiding the word "war."
5. commemorates (verb): To remember and show respect for a person, event, or group, often with a ceremony.

Grammar Focus: Passive Voice
The passive voice is used when the focus is on the action itself and who or what is affected by it, rather than on who performed the action. We often use it when the doer of the action is unknown, unimportant, or obvious, or when we want to make the object of an action more prominent. It is formed with a form of 'to be' + the past participle.
Example from the text: "However, this year's parade was noticeably scaled back compared to previous celebrations." (Here, the focus is on the parade being scaled back, not necessarily on who scaled it back.)

📅 May 09, 2026 - Analyzing Putin's Victory Day Message
This lesson explores the key messages from Russia's Victory Day parade speech and analyzes the language used.

Intermediate ESL: Understanding News Headlines

I. The News Brief

The Russian leader, Vladimir Putin, used his annual speech at the Victory Day parade to justify his so-called special military operation in Ukraine. This year's parade was noticeably scaled-back compared to previous years, reflecting the ongoing conflict. During his address, Putin strongly denounced NATO, a Western military alliance, and reiterated his view that the military actions were necessary for Russia's security. He presented the conflict as a defensive measure against perceived threats from the West. The speech also paid tribute to Russian soldiers currently fighting, aiming to strengthen national unity and support for the intervention among the Russian public.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. denounces (verb): publicly states that someone or something is bad or wrong.
2. scaled-back (adjective): made smaller or less intense than originally planned.
3. justify (verb): to give a good reason or explanation for something.
4. special military operation (noun phrase): a term used by Russia to refer to its war in Ukraine.

Grammar Focus: Reporting Verbs
Reporting verbs tell us what someone said, but they also give us more information about their attitude or the purpose of their words. For example, 'said' is neutral, but 'denounces' shows strong disapproval, and 'justify' means to give reasons. These verbs help convey the speaker's intentions and feelings. In the text, we see: "Putin denounces Nato" and "used his annual speech to justify his so-called special military operation."
