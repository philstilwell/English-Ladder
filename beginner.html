📅 May 27, 2026 [0 days, 0 hours old] - News from Lebanon: Israel Strikes and Vows to Crush Hezbollah
Israel has attacked places in Lebanon and says it will defeat Hezbollah.

Beginner ESL: Conflict in the Middle East: Israel, Lebanon, and Hezbollah

I. The News Brief

Dozens of people died in Lebanon. Israel attacked many places there. These attacks are called strikes. Israel says it struck 100 Hezbollah infrastructure sites. They also attacked Hezbollah fighters. Israel's Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu spoke. He made a vow to crush Hezbollah. This means he promised to defeat the group completely. Israel intensifies its strikes. More attacks happen now.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. infrastructure sites (noun phrase): Buildings, roads, and systems needed for a country or organization to work.
2. fighters (noun): People who fight in a war or conflict.
3. crush (verb): To defeat completely or destroy something.
4. intensifies (verb): Makes something stronger or more extreme.
5. vow (noun): A serious promise.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the Simple Past tense to talk about actions that finished in the past. We often add '-ed' to regular verbs. For irregular verbs, the form changes. For example, 'make' becomes 'made', 'die' becomes 'died', 'strike' becomes 'struck', and 'happen' becomes 'happened'.
Example from the text: "Dozens of people died in Lebanon."

📅 May 25, 2026 [1 day, 12 hours old] - Iran Says US Deal Not Imminent
Iran stated that an agreement with the US will not happen quickly.

Beginner ESL: International Relations

I. The News Brief

Iran made an important statement. They said a deal with the United States is not imminent. Imminent means it will not happen very soon. The US secretary of state spoke before this news. He said an agreement could possibly come on Monday. But Iran says this is not true now. So, there is no deal happening on Monday. Both countries want an agreement for peace. However, negotiations take a long time. This news means more talks are needed between them.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. statement (noun): Something a person or country says officially to explain something.
2. imminent (adjective): Happening very soon; about to happen.
3. secretary of state (noun phrase): A high-level government official who handles foreign affairs and relations with other countries.
4. possibly (adverb): Maybe; perhaps; it could happen but is not certain.
5. agreement (noun): A decision or promise made by two or more sides; a formal understanding.

Grammar Focus: Modal Verb: Could
We use the modal verb 'could' to talk about something that is possible in the future or in the past. It shows a possibility, but not a certainty that something will happen.
Example from the text: "an agreement could possibly come on Monday."

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions or events that started and finished in the past. For regular verbs, we often add '-ed'. For irregular verbs, the form changes. For example, 'to be' in the past becomes 'was' or 'were'.
