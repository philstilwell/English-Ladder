📅 May 09, 2026 - Putin's Victory Day Speech: Justifying Action
This lesson explores a recent news brief about Putin's Victory Day speech, focusing on key vocabulary, grammar, and comprehension.

Intermediate ESL: Understanding News Reports

I. The News Brief

On May 9th, Russia held its traditional Victory Day parade in Moscow's Red Square. However, this year's celebration was noticeably scaled-back compared to previous years. Russian President Vladimir Putin delivered his annual speech during the event. In his address, President Putin strongly denounces Nato, blaming Western nations for escalating global tensions. He claimed that the alliance poses a serious threat to Russia's security and sovereignty. The primary purpose of his speech was to justify what he calls the special military operation in Ukraine. Putin asserted that Russia was forced to act to protect its interests and people. He accused Ukraine of being controlled by external forces and preparing an attack. Many international observers, however, view these claims as baseless propaganda. The speech aimed to reinforce national unity and support for his policies among the Russian public.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. denounces (verb): openly criticizes something or someone strongly and publicly.
2. scaled-back (adjective): made smaller or less intense than originally planned.
3. annual speech (noun phrase): a speech that happens once every year.
4. justify (verb): to show that something is reasonable, right, or necessary.
5. special military operation (noun phrase): a term used by Russia to describe its invasion of Ukraine.

Grammar Focus: Reporting Verbs
Reporting verbs are used to tell someone what another person said, thought, or felt, often without using their exact words. Common reporting verbs include 'say', 'tell', 'ask', 'claim', 'assert', and 'accuse'. They are usually followed by 'that' + a clause, or an infinitive, or a gerund, depending on the verb.
Example from the text:
He claimed that the alliance poses a serious threat to Russia's security and sovereignty.
Putin asserted that Russia was forced to act to protect its interests and people.

This lesson explores a news brief about a recent speech by Vladimir Putin, focusing on key vocabulary, reporting verbs, and comprehension.

Intermediate ESL: Reporting on Current Events

I. The News Brief

Russian President Vladimir Putin delivered a speech at the annual Victory Day parade held in Moscow. This year's event was notably scaled back compared to previous years, drawing international attention. During his address, Putin strongly denounced NATO, the powerful military alliance, claiming they were creating threats near Russia's borders. The Russian leader also used this important national occasion to justify his ongoing "special military operation" in Ukraine. He stated that Russia was fighting against what he called 'neo-Nazism' and defending its vital security interests. Putin further explained that Russia needed to prevent a global war, presenting his actions as defensive. II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. denounce (verb): to strongly criticize someone or something publicly.
2. scaled-back (adjective): made smaller or less extensive than originally planned.
3. parade (noun): a public procession, often with music and costumes, celebrating a special day or event.
4. justify (verb): to give or be a good reason for something.
5. special military operation (noun phrase): a term used by Russia to describe its invasion of Ukraine, avoiding the word 'war'.

Grammar Focus: Reporting Verbs
Reporting verbs allow us to tell people what someone else said or thought, without necessarily quoting their exact words. They often introduce a 'that' clause (though 'that' can be optional). Common reporting verbs include 'say', 'tell', 'ask', 'state', 'claim', 'explain', and 'denounce'. Using different reporting verbs can show the speaker's attitude or purpose. For example, 'denounce' shows strong criticism, while 'claim' suggests something is stated but not necessarily proven.
Example from the text: "He claimed that NATO members were creating threats near Russia's borders." Here, 'claimed' reports Putin's assertion without confirming its truth.
