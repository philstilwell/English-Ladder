Officials believe his death will significantly weaken IS operations and morale in various regions. International security analysts are now assessing the broader implications of this strategic success in the ongoing fight against terrorism.

It is often used when the agent is unknown, unimportant, or obvious, or when we want to be more formal or objective.

Intermediate ESL: International Diplomacy and Trade Negotiations Between the US and China

I. The News Brief

President Trump and President Xi of China recently held high-level talks over two days. The leaders met to discuss various aspects of their countries' complex relationship, especially regarding trade. Despite initial expectations, official statements indicated a definite lack of concrete trade breakthroughs. Many observers noted that the meetings featured numerous choreographed ceremonies designed for public appearance. These public displays suggested an effort to maintain positive diplomatic relations between the two global powers. However, the core purpose of these discussions was to address ongoing trade imbalances and existing disputes. President Trump and President Xi did officially conclude their discussions, describing them as 'very successful.' This positive phrasing implies goodwill, even without specific agreements being made immediately public. The lack of specific deals announced means that significant trade issues likely remain unresolved between the two nations. Future negotiations will undoubtedly be necessary to achieve any major progress on the economic front.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. conclude (verb): To bring something to an end or finish it; to finalize a discussion or meeting.
2. talks (noun): Formal discussions or negotiations between people or groups, often about important matters.
3. breakthroughs (noun): Important discoveries or developments that help to solve a problem or open up new possibilities.
4. choreographed ceremonies (noun phrase): Carefully planned and arranged public events with specific movements or actions, often for symbolic purposes.
5. announced (verb): Made public or officially declared; stated something to a wide audience.

Grammar Focus: Reduced Relative Clauses (with Past Participles)
A reduced relative clause can simplify sentences by omitting the relative pronoun (like 'which' or 'that') and the verb 'to be' when they modify a noun. This is common with past participles (e.g., 'announced') to describe something that has undergone an action, focusing on the result. For example, 'deals that were announced' becomes 'deals announced,' making the sentence more concise.
Example from the text: "The lack of specific deals announced means that significant trade issues likely remain unresolved between the two nations."

📅 May 13, 2026 [2 days, 22 hours old] - German Court Rules Milka 'Shrinkflation' Tricked Consumers
A German court recently ruled that the manufacturer of the Milka Alpine Milk chocolate bar misled consumers by reducing its size without a clear price adjustment, a practice known as 'shrinkflation'.

Intermediate ESL: Consumer Rights, Shrinkflation, and Legal Rulings

I. The News Brief

A German court in Bremen recently made a significant decision concerning consumer protection against misleading business practices. The manufacturer of the popular Milka Alpine Milk chocolate bar was found guilty of 'shrinkflation'. This term refers to the practice where companies reduce the size or quantity of a product while keeping its price the same, effectively increasing the unit cost. Consumers often feel tricked by such changes, as they may not immediately notice the smaller packaging. The Bremen court concluded that the chocolate bar’s manufacturer did not adequately inform customers about the weight reduction. Specifically, the Milka Alpine Milk bar was reduced from 100 grams to 87 grams, leading to a considerable increase in price per kilogram. This ruling highlights the importance of transparency from manufacturers regarding product alterations. Consumer protection groups had brought the case forward, arguing that the company had deliberately misled shoppers. The court's verdict emphasizes that businesses must clearly communicate any changes in product size or quantity to avoid deceiving the public. This case sets a precedent, reinforcing consumer rights and potentially impacting how other companies approach product adjustments in the future.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. significant (adjective): Important or noticeable; having a special meaning or importance.
2. manufacturer (noun): A person or company that makes goods, especially on a large scale, using machinery.
3. guilty (adjective): Having committed a crime or wrong, or legally judged to have done so.
4. transparency (noun): The quality of being open, honest, and easy to understand; when information is clear and not hidden.
5. precedent (noun): An earlier event or action that is regarded as an example or guide to be considered in similar future circumstances.

Grammar Focus: The Passive Voice
The passive voice is used when the focus is on the action rather than the person or thing performing the action. It's often used when the doer is unknown, unimportant, or obvious from the context. It is formed with a form of 'to be' + the past participle of the main verb. For example, instead of 'Someone found the manufacturer guilty,' we say 'The manufacturer was found guilty' to emphasize the finding, not who found them guilty.
Example from the text: "The manufacturer of the popular Milka Alpine Milk chocolate bar was found guilty of 'shrinkflation'."

Intermediate ESL: International Relations and Conflict Resolution

I. The News Brief

President Trump recently made strong comments regarding the ongoing situation between the US and Iran. He stated that the ceasefire, intended to halt hostilities, is currently on 'massive life support'. This phrase suggests the ceasefire is in a very critical state and might not last much longer. The US president also openly criticized an Iranian counteroffer that aimed to end the conflict. According to Trump, this specific counteroffer was not sufficient to resolve the war. Furthermore, he described the month-long ceasefire as 'unbelievably weak,' expressing deep dissatisfaction. His remarks indicate a lack of confidence in the current peace efforts and the proposed solutions. The ceasefire had been established for a month, but its effectiveness is now severely questioned. This situation highlights the significant challenges in achieving a lasting peace agreement through diplomatic efforts. International observers are closely watching to see if further discussions can still save the ceasefire.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. ceasefire (noun): An agreement, usually between two armies or groups of people, to stop fighting for a period of time.
2. massive life support (idiom): A strong expression meaning something is in a very weak and critical condition, barely continuing to exist or function.
3. criticized (verb): To express disapproval of someone or something, usually by pointing out faults or mistakes.
4. counteroffer (noun): An offer made in response to a previous offer, often with different terms or conditions.
5. diplomatic efforts (noun phrase): Actions taken by representatives of governments to discuss and resolve issues peacefully, often involving negotiations.

Grammar Focus: Reporting Verbs
Reporting verbs are used to tell us what someone else said or thought, without directly quoting their exact words. They often introduce reported speech and can convey the speaker's attitude (e.g., 'stated' is neutral, 'criticized' shows disapproval). Common reporting verbs include say, tell, ask, state, report, describe, and explain.
