The Present Perfect Passive is formed with 'has/have been' + the past participle of the main verb. It is often used in news to report events where the agent is unknown, unimportant, or the focus is on the result.
Example from the text: "Mojtaba Khamenei, who is a prominent figure in Iran, has not been seen publicly for some time."

This ongoing pattern of severe heat raises serious concerns about public health and essential infrastructure. Authorities are advising citizens to take precautions, such as staying hydrated and avoiding direct sun exposure during the hottest parts of the day. The situation highlights the critical need for effective strategies to manage the impacts of future heatwaves.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. heatwave (noun): A period of unusually hot weather that lasts for several days or longer, often causing discomfort or danger.
2. excess deaths (noun phrase): The number of deaths above what would normally be expected for a given period, often linked to a specific event like a public health crisis or extreme weather.
3. forecasters (noun): People whose job is to predict future events or conditions, especially related to the weather.
4. brace for (phrasal verb): To prepare yourself for something difficult or unpleasant that is going to happen.
5. extreme temperatures (noun phrase): Temperatures that are much higher or lower than average, often reaching dangerous levels.
