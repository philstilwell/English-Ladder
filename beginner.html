📅 May 16, 2026 [0 days, 0 hours old] - US and Nigeria Eliminate Senior Leader
The US and Nigeria worked together to stop a dangerous leader.

Beginner ESL: International Cooperation Against Terrorism

I. The News Brief

A very important leader died. The United States and Nigeria helped. They did a special job together. This special job was an operation. President Donald Trump talked about it. He said the operation was good. The leader's name was Abu-Bilal al-Minuki. He was a very active terrorist. The operation killed this terrorist. So, the dangerous leader was eliminated.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. leader (noun): A person who guides or controls a group.
2. operation (noun): A planned action involving many people to achieve a goal.
3. killed (verb): To cause someone to die.
4. terrorist (noun): A person who uses violence to get political goals.
5. eliminated (verb): To completely remove or get rid of something.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that finished in the past. We often add '-ed' to regular verbs. For irregular verbs, the form changes. For example, 'talked' is the past of 'talk'.
Example from the text: "President Donald Trump talked about it."

Beginner ESL: International Relations and Trade

📅 May 13, 2026 [2 days, 22 hours old] - Milka Chocolate Bar Shrinks: A German Court Says 'Guilty' of Shrinkflation
A German court found the Milka chocolate manufacturer guilty because the chocolate bar became smaller but the price stayed the same.

Beginner ESL: Business News and Consumer Rights

I. The News Brief

Milka is a classic chocolate bar from Germany. But recently, the size of this chocolate bar changed. The manufacturer made the bar smaller than before. However, the price of the chocolate stayed the same. Many consumers bought the chocolate and noticed the change. They felt tricked because the bar shrank but the price did not. This change is an example of 'shrinkflation'. A German court in Bremen looked at this problem. The court found the manufacturer guilty of unfair practice. So, the company might need to make the bar bigger again.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. classic (adjective): Something that is very good and popular for a long time.
2. manufacturer (noun): A company or person that makes goods or products.
3. shrank (verb (past simple)): The past form of 'shrink', meaning to become smaller in size.
4. consumers (noun): People who buy and use products and services.
5. guilty (adjective): Responsible for doing something wrong or for a crime.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that finished at a specific time in the past. Many regular verbs add '-ed' (e.g., looked). Irregular verbs change their form (e.g., 'find' becomes 'found', 'make' becomes 'made', 'shrink' becomes 'shrank').
Example from the text: "The court found the manufacturer guilty of unfair practice."
