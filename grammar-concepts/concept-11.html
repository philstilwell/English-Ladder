Grammar Concepts #11

Understanding “Go/Come” and “Bring/Take”

  • Detailed Examples Based on the Image

Core Idea

When you communicate with friends on the phone or by email, it’s important to use go/come and take/bring correctly. These words depend on the speaker’s and listener’s locations and perspectives.

Key Points

Go vs. Come

  • Go: Used when the movement is away from the speaker’s location.
  • Come: Used when the movement is towards the speaker’s location.

Examples:

  • Go:
  • “I will go to the store.”
  • “She goes to school every day.”
  • “We are going to the park this afternoon.”
  • Come:
  • “Can you come to my house?”
  • “She came to the office early.”
  • “They are coming to the party tonight.”

Bring vs. Take

  • Bring: Used when the object is moved towards the speaker or listener.
  • Take: Used when the object is moved away from the speaker or listener.

Examples:

  • Bring:
  • “Please bring your book to class.”
  • “He will bring a gift to the party.”
  • “Can you bring me some water?”
  • Take:
  • “Take this document to the manager.”
  • “She took her kids to the zoo.”
  • “I will take the trash out.”

Detailed Examples Based on the Image

Linda Tells Mike:

  • Context: Linda is with Mike, talking about Nancy’s party.
  • “I’m going (there) to Nancy’s party tonight.” Explanation: Linda is moving away from her current location (where she is with Mike) to Nancy’s party.
  • “I don’t want to take you (there) since you’re grumpy.” Explanation: Linda is thinking of moving Mike away from their current location (where she is with Mike) to Nancy’s party.

Linda Tells Nancy:

  • Context: Linda is talking to Nancy, who is at her party.
  • “I’m coming (there) to your party tonight.” Explanation: Linda is moving towards Nancy’s location (the party).
  • “I don’t want to bring Mike (there). He’s grumpy.” Explanation: Linda is thinking of moving Mike towards Nancy’s location (the party).

Nancy Tells Linda:

  • Context: Nancy is at her party, talking to Linda.
  • “I’m glad you’re coming (here) to my party tonight.” Explanation: Nancy appreciates that Linda is moving towards her location (the party).
  • “Bring Mike (here) another time.” Explanation: Nancy suggests that Linda should move Mike towards her location (the party) at another time.

Additional Example Sentences

Go:

  • “We will go to the beach tomorrow.”
  • “He goes to the gym every morning.”
  • “They are going to visit their grandparents.”

Come:

  • “Can you come to my office at 3 PM?”
  • “She came to the meeting late.”
  • “They are coming to see the new movie.”

Bring:

  • “Bring your umbrella; it’s going to rain.”
  • “He always brings snacks to the meetings.”
  • “Can you bring your laptop to the workshop?”

Take:

  • “I took my dog for a walk.”
  • “She took her cat to the vet.”
  • “They will take the bus to the city center.”

Summary

Understanding the correct usage of go/come and take/bring is crucial for clear communication. Remember:

  • Use go and take when moving away from the current location.
  • Use come and bring when moving towards the current location.

By practicing these concepts with various sentences, you can improve your understanding and usage in everyday conversations.
