📅 July 12, 2026 [0 days, 0 hours old] - US Launches Strikes as Iran Closes Strait of Hormuz
The US recently launched attacks because of an incident involving a ship near Iran.

Beginner ESL: International shipping and country actions

I. The News Brief

The US launches new strikes. These strikes are happening after an attack. A ship, or vessel, was attacked. This vessel had a flag from Cyprus. It was moving in an important waterway. This waterway is the Strait of Hormuz. Iran sometimes closes this Strait. The US launches the strikes because of the attack on the vessel. The waterway is critical for many ships. This area is important for international travel.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. launches (verb): starts or sends out something new, like an attack.
2. strikes (noun): attacks, usually by military planes or missiles.
3. vessel (noun): a ship or large boat.
4. waterway (noun): a path over water where ships can travel.
5. closes (verb): shuts something, so nothing can go through.

Grammar Focus: Using "because" to show reason
We use "because" to explain why something happens. It tells us the reason for an action. For example, "I am happy because it is sunny." The sun is the reason I am happy.
Example from the text: "The US launches the strikes because of the attack on the vessel."

📅 July 07, 2026 [4 days, 22 hours old] - Zelensky Asks Nato for More Air Defence
Ukrainian President Zelensky will ask Nato for more air defence systems in Turkey.

Beginner ESL: Zelensky's Request for Air Defence

President Zelensky is from Ukraine. He wants to speak with Nato. Nato is a group of countries. Zelensky will go to a meeting in Turkey. He will ask for help there. Ukraine needs more air defence systems. Russia made intense strikes on Ukraine. These strikes used missiles. Ukraine needs interceptor missiles. These missiles can stop Russian attacks.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

2. ask for (verb phrase): To say you want or need something from another person or group.
3. air defence systems (noun phrase): Special machines or weapons that protect a place from attacks coming from the sky, like planes or missiles.
4. intense strikes (noun phrase): Very strong and heavy attacks, often with many bombs or missiles.
5. interceptor missiles (noun phrase): Special rockets that are launched to hit and destroy other enemy rockets or aircraft in the air.

Grammar Focus: Future with 'will'
We use 'will' to talk about things that we plan to do or expect to happen in the future. It helps us speak about actions or decisions that have not happened yet.
Example from the text: "Zelensky will go to a meeting in Turkey."
