This structure adds sophistication and conciseness to sentences.

Advanced Grammar: Reduced Relative Clauses
Reduced relative clauses (also known as participial phrases) are a way to make sentences more concise by omitting the relative pronoun (e.g., who, which, that) and the auxiliary verb (e.g., is, are, was, were) from an active or passive relative clause. For passive clauses, the past participle is used directly. For active clauses, the present participle (-ing form) is used. They function as adjectives, providing additional information about a noun without forming a separate clause.
