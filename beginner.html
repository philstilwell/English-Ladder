📅 May 20, 2026 [0 days, 0 hours old] - Ebola Virus Spreads in DR Congo
Ebola is a dangerous virus that is spreading in eastern DR Congo.

Beginner ESL: Health Crisis: Ebola Outbreak in DR Congo

I. The News Brief

Ebola is a very serious virus. It is spreading in eastern DR Congo. People feel a lot of fear because of the virus. The health minister talked about the situation. He said doctors and nurses are working hard. They are trying to stop the virus. Medics were slow to detect Ebola at first. Now they are playing catch-up with the virus. The virus has tortured many people there. They want to stop the deadly virus from spreading more.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. virus (noun): A very small thing that can make people sick.
2. spreading (verb): Moving to new places and affecting more people.
3. fear (noun): A bad feeling when you think something dangerous will happen.
4. health minister (noun phrase): A government official in charge of a country's health.
5. medics (noun): Doctors, nurses, and other health workers.

Grammar Focus: Using 'because' to explain a reason
We use the word 'because' to show the reason for something. It connects an action or feeling to why it happens. For example, 'I am happy because it is sunny.'
Example from the text: "People feel a lot of fear because of the virus."

📅 May 19, 2026 [0 days, 12 hours old] - Ebola Outbreak: Fear Spreads in Central Africa
People are afraid because a dangerous disease called Ebola is spreading quickly in central Africa.

Beginner ESL: Ebola outbreak and fear in central Africa

I. The News Brief

Ebola is a very serious sickness. Many people in central Africa have this disease. Doctors think hundreds of people are sick. This outbreak of Ebola is dangerous. Experts fear the real number of sick people is much higher. The disease is spreading very quickly. People say Ebola has tortured them. They are very worried now. 3. higher (adjective): A larger amount or number.
4. spreading (verb (present participle)): Moving and affecting more places or people.
5. tortured (verb (past participle)): Caused a lot of pain and suffering.

Grammar Focus: Present Continuous
We use the present continuous to talk about actions happening now or around now. We make it with 'be' (am, is, are) + verb-ing.
Example from the text: "The disease is spreading very quickly."

📅 May 17, 2026 [2 days, 14 hours old] - Ebola Outbreak Declared Global Health Emergency
The World Health Organization says the Ebola outbreak in DR Congo is now a global health emergency.

Beginner ESL: Public Health and Disease Outbreaks

I. The News Brief

The World Health Organization (WHO) helps people stay healthy. They made an important declaration about Ebola. Ebola is a very serious illness that makes people sick. There is an outbreak of Ebola in DR Congo. An outbreak means many people get sick in one area. The WHO now says this is a global health emergency. This means the problem needs help from many countries. About 246 people have caught the illness in DR Congo. Sadly, around 80 people have died because of this sickness. The WHO did not call it a pandemic emergency.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. declaration (noun): an official and important statement
2. outbreak (noun): when a disease suddenly begins to happen to many people at the same time
3. global health emergency (phrase): a very serious health problem that needs urgent action and help from countries all over the world
4. sickness (noun): the state of being ill; a disease or illness
5. pandemic emergency (phrase): a very serious disease that spreads quickly across many countries or continents, causing a widespread health crisis

Grammar Focus: There is / There are
We use 'There is' when we talk about one thing existing. We use 'There are' when we talk about more than one thing existing. It introduces what is present.
Example from the text: "There is an outbreak of Ebola in DR Congo."

'But' is a word we use to show a difference or a contrast between two ideas. It connects two parts of a sentence when the second part says something different from the first part.

📅 May 14, 2026 [5 days, 13 hours old] - US and China Leaders Meet for High-Stakes Talks
Leaders from the US and China are meeting to discuss important topics like trade, Iran, and Taiwan.

Beginner ESL: International Relations and Trade

I. The News Brief

President Trump arrives in China for very important discussions. These discussions are called high-stakes talks. Trump wants China to open up its markets for more business. To "open up" means to make something more accessible or available. China is also expected to talk about Taiwan. Taiwan is a sensitive topic between the two countries. The leaders will discuss global trade matters. They will also discuss issues related to Iran. These talks are very important for the future of both countries. Both nations hope to find agreements during these meetings.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. arrives (verb): comes to a place, especially at the end of a journey.
2. talks (noun): formal discussions or meetings between people or groups, often to solve problems.
3. open up (phrasal verb): to make something more accessible, available, or free to enter, often for business.
4. trade (noun): the activity of buying, selling, or exchanging goods and services between people or countries.
5. important (adjective): having great value, meaning, or significance; essential.

Grammar Focus: Simple Present Tense for Scheduled Events
We use the simple present tense to talk about actions that are planned or scheduled for the future, like a timetable, an official visit, or an important arrival. It shows that the event is definite.
Example from the text: "President Trump arrives in China for very important discussions."
