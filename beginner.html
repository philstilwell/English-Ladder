📅 July 05, 2026 [0 days, 0 hours old] - The US Celebrates Its 250th Birthday
The United States celebrated its 250th birthday with many events.

Beginner ESL: US Independence Day Celebration

I. The News Brief

The United States celebrated its 250th birthday. There were big parties across the country. People saw fireworks in the sky. Military planes did flyovers. President Trump gave a speech. His speech talked about war veterans. He also spoke about American history. Some parts of his speech were about politics. The celebrations happened with some extreme weather. This was a special day for the US.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. celebrated (verb): had a special party for an important day.
2. birthday (noun): the day a person or country was born.
3. speech (noun): a formal talk given to a group of people.
4. veterans (noun): people who were soldiers in a war.
5. history (noun): all the important things that happened in the past.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that finished in the past. We often add '-ed' to regular verbs. For irregular verbs, the form changes. This tense tells us when something happened and it is now over.
Example from the text: "The United States celebrated its 250th birthday."

I. The News Brief

These temperatures will come in the next few days. The continent of Europe will feel this hot weather.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

2. excess deaths (noun phrase): More people died than normal at that time.
3. peak (noun): The highest or strongest point of something.
4. forecasters (noun): People who say what the weather will be like in the future.
5. extreme temperatures (noun phrase): Weather that is much hotter or colder than normal.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the Simple Past tense to talk about actions that finished in the past. It tells us about things that already happened. We often add '-ed' to regular verbs or use special forms for irregular verbs.
Example from the text: "More than 2,000 extra people died in France then."
