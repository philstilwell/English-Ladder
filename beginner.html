📅 May 09, 2026 - News from Russia
Learn about a news event from Russia and practice English words and grammar.

Beginner ESL: News About Russia

I. The News Brief

It was a special day in Russia called Victory Day. Mr. Putin is the leader of Russia. He gave a speech at a parade. The parade was smaller than usual. Mr. Putin talked about the conflict in Ukraine. He said Russia's actions in Ukraine were necessary.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. Leader (noun): The person who guides a country or group.
2. Speech (noun): When someone talks to many people.
3. Parade (noun): Many people walk or cars drive in a line for a special day.
4. Smaller than usual (phrase): Less big than it normally is.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that finished before now. It tells us about something that already happened.
Example from the text: "He gave a speech at a parade." ('gave' is the simple past of 'give').

📅 May 09, 2026 - News About a Special Day
This lesson is about a leader's speech and a special day in Russia.

Beginner ESL: A Speech on Victory Day

I. The News Brief

It was Victory Day in Russia. Many people celebrate this day. There was a parade in the city. The parade was not very big this year. President Putin is the leader of Russia. He gave a speech. In his speech, he talked about why the military operation in Ukraine is important.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. Victory Day (noun): A special day to remember a big win or success.
2. parade (noun): A celebration where people walk or ride in a line, often with music.
3. leader (noun): The person who guides a country or a group of people.
4. speech (noun): When one person talks to many people, usually for an important reason.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that happened before now. For example, "was" is the past of "is," and "gave" is the past of "give."
Example from the text: "President Putin gave a speech." (The action of giving the speech happened in the past.)

III. Comprehension & Mastery Quiz

1. What special day was it?

2. What did President Putin do?

3. Where was the parade?

4. What does "leader" mean?

5. Was the parade big this year?

6. When did President Putin give his speech?

7. What did President Putin talk about in his speech?
