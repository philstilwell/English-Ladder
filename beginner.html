Beginner ESL: International Relations and Diplomacy: The US and Iran

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

4. doubt (verb): To feel unsure or not believe something is true.
5. timing (noun): The chosen moment or schedule for something to happen.

Grammar Focus: Simple Present (Reporting what someone says)
We use the Simple Present to talk about facts or to report what someone says right now. We often use verbs like 'says' or 'talks' in this way.
Example from the text: "President Trump talks about a deal."

📅 June 11, 2026 [2 days, 21 hours old] - El Niño Begins: Global Weather Changes Expected
An El Niño event has officially started, bringing fears of extreme weather and higher temperatures worldwide.

Beginner ESL: El Niño and Global Climate

I. The News Brief

An El Niño event is a natural change in the ocean and air. It happens in the Pacific Ocean near the equator. US scientists say this El Niño event has officially started. This means the event is now active and has been confirmed. El Niño can cause big changes in weather around the world. It often brings extreme weather, like very heavy rain or drought in different regions. We can expect higher temperatures in many places on Earth. These changes can make some areas much hotter than normal. People should prepare for possible weather difficulties and challenges. It is a significant natural global weather pattern.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. El Niño event (noun phrase): A natural change in the ocean and air that affects global weather patterns.
2. officially started (verb phrase): It has begun and is confirmed by recognized authorities or experts.
3. US scientists (noun phrase): Experts from America who study science, especially weather and climate.
4. extreme weather (noun phrase): Weather that is very strong, severe, or unusual, like floods, droughts, or heatwaves.
5. higher temperatures (noun phrase): When the air or water becomes hotter or warmer than it normally is.

Grammar Focus: Simple Present Tense (Verb 'to be')
We use the simple present tense of the verb 'to be' (is, am, are) to talk about facts, general truths, and things that happen regularly. It describes something that is true now or always.
Example from the text: "An El Niño event is a natural change in the ocean and air."
