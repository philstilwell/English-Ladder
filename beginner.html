📅 July 22, 2026 [0 days, 0 hours old] - Ukrainian Drones Target Russian Online Retailer Wildberries
Ukrainian drones recently attacked storage buildings belonging to the Russian online company Wildberries.

Beginner ESL: News of Drone Attacks and Online Retailers

I. The News Brief

Ukrainian drones flew into Russia. They hit some buildings. These buildings belong to Wildberries. Wildberries is a Russian online retailer. The drones struck logistics hubs. Logistics hubs help store and send many products. This attack happened overnight. It was in two regions of Russia. One region is called Krasnodar. The other region is Stavropol.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. drones (noun): Flying machines that can be controlled from far away without a person inside.
2. hit (verb): To strike something or make strong contact with it, often with force.
3. online retailer (noun): A business that sells products directly to customers over the internet.
4. logistics hubs (noun): Large centers where goods are organized, stored, and prepared for shipping and delivery.
5. overnight (adverb): During the entire night, from evening until the next morning.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the Simple Past Tense to talk about actions or events that finished at a specific time in the past. We often add '-ed' to regular verbs, but many common verbs have irregular past tense forms.
Example from the text: "This attack happened overnight."

📅 July 21, 2026 [1 day, 0 hours old] - USA Puts High Taxes on Canadian Products: Trade Problems Grow

Beginner ESL: International Trade Disputes and Tariffs

I. The News Brief

President Trump made a new decision. He put new taxes on goods from Canada. These taxes are called tariffs. The tariffs are very high, 50%. This is a big problem for trade. Trade tensions are growing between the two countries. Canada and the USA are North American neighbours. Mark Carney wants to talk more. He wants to intensify trade talks. They hope to solve the trade issues soon.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. tariffs (noun): Special taxes on things that countries buy from other countries.
2. goods (noun): Things that people make or grow to sell, like cars or food.
3. trade (noun): The buying and selling of goods and services between people or countries.
4. tensions (noun): A feeling of anger or disagreement between people or countries.
5. intensify (verb): To make something stronger or more serious.

Grammar Focus: Simple Present Tense
We use the simple present tense for things that happen regularly, or for facts and general truths. It describes what is true now. For example, 'The sun rises every day.'
Example from the text: "The tariffs are very high, 50%."

📅 July 18, 2026 [4 days, 0 hours old] - US Strikes and Iran's Response: A News Brief

Beginner ESL: International Conflict and Retaliation

I. The News Brief

These helping countries are called US allies. These events are happening in the Middle East region. Kuwait is one of these US allies. Kuwait said that a power and water plant was hit. This means a factory that makes electricity and water was attacked. There is a lot of tension in the region because of these actions.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. consecutive (adjective): Happening one after another without any break.
2. retaliated (verb): To fight back or attack someone because they attacked you first.
3. allies (noun): Countries or groups that work together and help each other, often in a war.
4. region (noun): A large area of land, usually without exact borders, like the Middle East.
5. plant (noun): A factory or a place where power, water, or other things are made.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that happened and finished at a specific time in the past. We often add '-ed' to regular verbs, or use different forms for irregular verbs. It tells us what occurred.
Example from the text: "Kuwait said that a power and water plant was hit."
