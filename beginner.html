Beginner ESL: Putin's Victory Day Speech

I. The News Brief

It was Victory Day in Russia on May 9. President Putin gave a speech. He spoke about his country's special military operation in Ukraine. He wanted to justify this operation to the people. Putin talked a lot about Nato. He said Nato was a big threat to Russia. He used his annual speech to explain his ideas. The speech happened at a parade. This year, the parade was scaled-back. Putin criticized Nato in his speech.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. special military operation (noun phrase): Russia's name for its war in Ukraine.
2. justify (verb): To give a good reason for an action.
3. Nato (proper noun): A group of countries that work to protect each other.
4. threat (noun): Something that can cause danger.
5. scaled-back (adjective): Smaller or less important than usual.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that happened and finished in the past. This news brief talks about past events. For example, 'President Putin gave a speech.' The action 'give' happened and finished. Many verbs in the news brief are in the simple past.
Example from the text: "President Putin gave a speech."
