📅 May 28, 2026 [0 days, 0 hours old] - Iran Targets American Base After US Strikes
There is new fighting between Iran and the US after a time of peace.

Beginner ESL: US-Iran Relations and Conflict

I. The News Brief

Iran said it targeted an American base. This attack happened after new strikes from the US. This news shows fighting has started again. Before, there was a fragile ceasefire. A ceasefire means a short time of peace. This peace was between the US and Iran. They were also talking to stop a three-month war. These talks were to end the fighting. The peace was not strong, it was fragile. So, the two countries are fighting again.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. targeted (verb): To aim at something or someone and attack it.
2. strikes (noun): Attacks, usually by military aircraft or bombs.
3. fighting (noun): A physical struggle or conflict between people or groups.
4. ceasefire (noun): A time when two groups stop fighting for a short period.
5. fragile (adjective): Easily broken or not strong.

Grammar Focus: Using 'after'
We use 'after' to show that one thing happens, and then another thing happens later. The second thing happens 'after' the first thing.
Example from the text: "This attack happened after new strikes from the US."

📅 May 27, 2026 [1 day, 0 hours old] - Ebola and Conflict in DR Congo
The World Health Organization warns that Ebola and fighting are a big problem in DR Congo.

Beginner ESL: Ebola disease and conflict in the Democratic Republic of Congo

I. The News Brief

DR Congo has a serious problem. Ebola is a dangerous disease there. There is also a lot of conflict. The fighting is hampering efforts to help sick people. The World Health Organization (WHO) helps DR Congo. WHO chief Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus spoke about this. He warns that the situation is very bad. It is a 'catastrophic collision' of problems. Workers cannot easily stop the spread of Ebola. The conflict makes health work very difficult.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. conflict (noun): A fight or a war between people or groups.
2. disease (noun): A sickness or illness that makes people or animals unwell.
3. warns (verb): Tells someone about a possible danger or problem.
4. hampering (verb): Making it difficult for something to happen or move easily.
5. spread (noun): The way something moves to many places or people.

Grammar Focus: The verb "to be" (is/are) in Simple Present
We use "is" for one thing or "are" for many things to talk about facts or what is true right now. For example, "Ebola is a disease" (one thing). "Problems are difficult" (many things).
Example from the text: "Ebola is a dangerous disease there."

📅 May 26, 2026 [1 day, 20 hours old] - Iran Condemns US Strikes: A Ceasefire Violation During Peace Talks

Beginner ESL: International Relations, Conflict, Ceasefire Violation

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. strikes (noun): Attacks, often by military, using bombs or missiles.
2. gross violation (noun phrase): A very serious breaking of a rule or an important agreement.
3. ceasefire (noun): An official agreement to stop fighting in a war or conflict for a period of time.
4. negotiators (noun): People who talk with others to try and reach an agreement or solve a problem.
5. peace talks (noun phrase): Discussions and meetings between groups or countries to try and stop a conflict and find a peaceful solution.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the Simple Past Tense to talk about actions that started and finished in the past. For many verbs, we add "-ed" to the end. For some irregular verbs, the word changes completely.
Example from the text: "Iran called them a "gross violation" of an agreement."

📅 May 25, 2026 [3 days, 0 hours old] - Iran Says US Deal Not Imminent
Iran stated that an agreement with the US will not happen quickly.

Beginner ESL: International Relations

I. The News Brief

Iran made an important statement. They said a deal with the United States is not imminent. Imminent means it will not happen very soon. The US secretary of state spoke before this news. He said an agreement could possibly come on Monday. But Iran says this is not true now. So, there is no deal happening on Monday. Both countries want an agreement for peace. However, negotiations take a long time. This news means more talks are needed between them.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. statement (noun): Something a person or country says officially to explain something.
2. imminent (adjective): Happening very soon; about to happen.
3. secretary of state (noun phrase): A high-level government official who handles foreign affairs and relations with other countries.
4. possibly (adverb): Maybe; perhaps; it could happen but is not certain.
5. agreement (noun): A decision or promise made by two or more sides; a formal understanding.

Grammar Focus: Modal Verb: Could
We use the modal verb 'could' to talk about something that is possible in the future or in the past. It shows a possibility, but not a certainty that something will happen.
Example from the text: "an agreement could possibly come on Monday."

📅 May 23, 2026 [5 days, 2 hours old] - Coal Mine Accident in China: 90 People Killed
A big explosion happened in a coal mine in China, and many people died.

Beginner ESL: Mining Disaster in China

I. The News Brief

There was a terrible accident in a coal mine in China. This happened in Shanxi province. A big explosion occurred inside the mine. Many people were working there. State media reported the news. At least 90 people were killed in the explosion. This is a very sad event for China. It is the country's worst mining disaster in 16 years. The government is investigating the cause of the blast. Safety in mines is very important to prevent such accidents.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. explosion (noun): a sudden, loud burst of something, often causing damage.
2. killed (verb (past simple)): caused someone to die.
3. state media (noun phrase): news organizations that are controlled or owned by the government.
4. disaster (noun): a very bad accident or event that causes a lot of damage, harm, or death.
5. province (noun): a large area or region within a country, similar to a state.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions or events that started and finished in the past. For regular verbs, we often add '-ed'. For irregular verbs, the form changes. For example, 'to be' in the past becomes 'was' or 'were'.
Example from the text: "At least 90 people were killed in the explosion."
