📅 June 06, 2026 [0 days, 0 hours old] - Ukraine Drones Attack St Petersburg
Ukrainian drones attacked St Petersburg, and Russia called it a big attack.

Beginner ESL: War in Ukraine and drone attacks

I. The News Brief

Ukraine sent many drones. The drones targeted St Petersburg in Russia. Russia said this attack was "unprecedented." "Unprecedented" means it was very unusual. The regional governor spoke to the people. He told residents to stay indoors. This was the first time for this warning. It happened since the war started. People needed to stay safe inside their homes. The drones flew a long way to reach the city.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. drones (noun): Small flying machines that do not have a person inside.
2. targeted (verb): To aim at something or someone specifically.
3. attack (noun): A violent act to hurt or damage something or someone.
4. residents (noun): People who live in a particular place or area.
5. indoors (adverb): Inside a building, not outside.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the Simple Past Tense to talk about actions that happened in the past and are now finished. For many verbs, we add "-ed" to the end. Some common verbs, like "say," change their form completely to show the past.
Example from the text: "Russia said this attack was "unprecedented.""

📅 June 03, 2026 [2 days, 20 hours old] - Drone Strikes: Iran and Kuwait News
Drone strikes happened at Kuwait airport, injuring many people.

Beginner ESL: International News: Middle East Conflict

I. The News Brief

Iran launched drone strikes on Kuwait. The strikes hit a main airport in Kuwait. One person was killed in the attack. Many people, about fifty, were injured. Iran said this was a retaliation for earlier attacks. This means Iran was striking back. Before this, the United States made strikes on Iran. The US strikes hit an Iranian oil tanker. They also attacked an island in Iran. Iran's actions happened because of the US attacks.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. drone strikes (noun phrase): Attacks using special flying machines without a pilot.
2. killed (verb (past tense)): To cause someone to die; past form of kill.
3. injured (verb (past tense)): To hurt a person or animal; past form of injure.
4. retaliation (noun): An action taken to harm someone because they harmed you first; fighting back.
5. oil tanker (noun): A very large ship that carries oil across the sea.

Grammar Focus: Using 'because of' to show reason
We use the words 'because of' to explain the reason or cause for something. It connects an action with why that action happened. For example, 'I am happy because of the sunny weather.'
Example from the text: "Iran's actions happened because of the US attacks."
