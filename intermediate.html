France recently experienced a severe heatwave resulting in thousands of excess deaths, while forecasters warn that Europe should prepare for even higher temperatures in the near future.

Intermediate ESL: Extreme Weather and Climate Change in Europe

I. The News Brief

France recently experienced a severe heatwave, leading to a significant rise in fatalities. Specifically, 2,025 excess deaths were recorded in the country during the peak of this extreme weather event. These figures represent more deaths than typically expected for that period, directly linked to the dangerously high temperatures. Meanwhile, forecasters are issuing urgent warnings across the continent about the developing weather patterns. Europe must now brace for even more extreme temperatures in the coming days, according to meteorological predictions. Weather models indicate that several countries will see temperatures soaring, potentially breaking new records. This ongoing pattern of severe heat raises serious concerns about public health and essential infrastructure. Authorities are advising citizens to take precautions, such as staying hydrated and avoiding direct sun exposure during the hottest parts of the day. Such prolonged periods of intense heat are becoming increasingly common due to the broader effects of climate change. The situation highlights the critical need for effective strategies to manage the impacts of future heatwaves.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. heatwave (noun): A period of unusually hot weather that lasts for several days or longer, often causing discomfort or danger.
2. excess deaths (noun phrase): The number of deaths above what would normally be expected for a given period, often linked to a specific event like a public health crisis or extreme weather.
3. forecasters (noun): People whose job is to predict future events or conditions, especially related to the weather.
4. brace for (phrasal verb): To prepare yourself for something difficult or unpleasant that is going to happen.
5. extreme temperatures (noun phrase): Temperatures that are much higher or lower than average, often reaching dangerous levels.

Grammar Focus: Passive Voice
The passive voice is used when the focus is on the action or the object of the action rather than the subject performing the action. We use 'to be' + past participle. It's common in news reports when the 'doer' of the action is unknown, unimportant, or obvious, or when we want to emphasize the result.
Example from the text: "Specifically, 2,025 excess deaths were recorded in the country during the peak of this extreme weather event."

📅 June 27, 2026 [6 days, 0 hours old] - US and Iran Exchange Strikes Following Cargo Ship Incident
Tensions have escalated between the US and Iran after American forces launched strikes in response to an attack on a cargo ship, leading Iran to accuse the US of violating a prior agreement and retaliate.

Intermediate ESL: International Relations and Military Actions

I. The News Brief

An attack on a cargo ship recently occurred, significantly increasing regional tensions. Following this incident, the United States launched military strikes against targets in Iran. These American actions were reportedly a direct response to the earlier cargo ship attack. However, Iran quickly accused the US of violating an existing agreement between the two nations. Iran stated that the US strikes violated an important understanding they had previously established. Furthermore, Iran declared that it had carried out its own counter-strikes. These Iranian military actions targeted sites connected to American forces. Iran explained its strikes were a direct retaliation, taking place "in response" to the US attacks. The escalating exchange of strikes highlights the delicate and strained relationship between Washington and Tehran. This cycle of accusations and military actions further complicates efforts for peace and stability in the region.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. cargo ship (noun phrase): A large vessel specifically designed for transporting goods and merchandise across seas or oceans.
2. strikes (noun): Military attacks, typically carried out by aircraft or missiles, targeting specific locations or forces.
3. accused (verb (past participle)): Charged someone with an offense or wrongdoing, often formally or publicly, without necessarily proving guilt.
4. violating (verb (present participle)): Breaking or failing to observe a rule, law, agreement, or principle, often resulting in a negative consequence.
5. in response (phrase): As a reaction to something that has happened or been said; acting directly because of an earlier event or action.

Grammar Focus: Reporting Verbs
Reporting verbs allow us to convey what someone else said, thought, or felt without directly quoting them. These verbs, like 'accuse,' 'state,' 'declare,' or 'explain,' often introduce a 'that' clause and help integrate information smoothly into a text, making writing more concise and sophisticated. They are essential for summarizing events or opinions from different sources.
Example from the text: "Iran stated that the US strikes violated an important understanding they had previously established."
