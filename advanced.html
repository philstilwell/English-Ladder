The verb 'risk' is a prime example, indicating the possibility or danger of a particular action or outcome occurring. This construction effectively conveys potential negative consequences.
Example from the text: "Such protectionist measures typically aim to safeguard domestic industries but often risk precipitating retaliatory actions from affected countries."

Advanced ESL: Geopolitical Escalation Between the United States and Iran in the Middle East

I. The News Brief

The Middle East is currently witnessing a protracted period of heightened tensions as the United States and Iran engage in a reciprocal cycle of military actions. This latest flurry of activity was precipitated by a drone attack in Jordan that tragically resulted in the deaths of two American servicemen. Reduced relative clauses are a sophisticated way to make sentences more concise by omitting the relative pronoun (e.g., 'who,' 'which,' 'that') and a form of 'be' (e.g., 'is,' 'are,' 'was,' 'were'). When the original clause is passive, a past participle (-ed form) is used directly after the noun, as seen in 'militias deemed responsible' which implies 'militias who are deemed responsible'.
Example from the text: "These strategic bombardments aim to degrade the operational capabilities of militias deemed responsible for aggressions against American personnel."
