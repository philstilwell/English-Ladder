Understanding Prepositions: in/on/at

Core Idea

Prepositions are words used to show the relationship of a noun or pronoun to other words in a sentence. They often indicate location, time, or direction. This guide will explain the use of the prepositions in, on, and at, which are commonly used to express these relationships.

General Rules

  • in: Used for the largest categories, such as cities, countries, months, and years.
  • on: Used for more specific items like streets, surfaces, and dates.
  • at: Used for the most specific points, such as exact locations, times, and specific places.

Location

in (Largest Category)

  • City/Country:
  • Example: “I was in New York.”
  • Example: “She lives in Japan.”
  • Building/Enclosed Space:
  • Example: “He is in the library.”
  • Example: “We met in the cafe.”

on (More Specific)

  • Streets:
  • Example: “I was on Wall Street.”
  • Example: “The store is on Main Street.”
  • Surfaces:
  • Example: “The book is on the table.”
  • Example: “There is a painting on the wall.”
  • Floors of a Building:
  • Example: “I was on the 12th floor.”
  • Example: “She works on the second floor.”

at (Most Specific)

  • Exact Locations:
  • Example: “I was at the Empire State Building.”
  • Example: “We will meet at the entrance.”
  • Specific Places within a Building:
  • Example: “I was at the elevator.”
  • Example: “He is at the reception desk.”

Time

in (Larger Periods)

  • Months/Years:
  • Example: “I was busy in February.”
  • Example: “She was born in 1990.”
  • Seasons:
  • Example: “We travel a lot in the summer.”
  • Example: “The flowers bloom in spring.”

on (Specific Days/Dates)

  • Days of the Week/Dates:
  • Example: “I will celebrate my birthday on April 2.”
  • Example: “We have a meeting on Monday.”
  • Specific Days/Events:
  • Example: “He arrived on Christmas Day.”
  • Example: “They left on New Year’s Eve.”

at (Exact Times)

  • Specific Times:
  • Example: “I will eat lunch at 11:45.”
  • Example: “The train arrives at 3:30 PM.”
  • Holiday Periods/Specific Moments:
  • Example: “We opened presents at midnight.”
  • Example: “She woke up at dawn.”

Information and Other Uses

in

  • Books/Media:
  • Example: “There is much information in the book.”
  • Example: “I read it in the newspaper.”

on

  • Pages/Sections:
  • Example: “There is more information on page 32.”
  • Example: “I saw it on the website.”

at

  • Specific Points in a Document:
  • Example: “The page number is at the top of the page.”
  • Example: “Look at the beginning of the chapter.”

Additional Examples

in

  • “I live in a big house.”
  • “They are in the car.”
  • “We will visit them in December.”

on

  • “She left her keys on the counter.”
  • “He wrote his name on the board.”
  • “The concert is on Friday.”

at

  • “He is at the supermarket.”
  • “She is waiting at the bus stop.”
  • “The meeting starts at noon.”

Understanding the use of in, on, and at can greatly improve your ability to express locations, times, and other relationships in English. Practice using these prepositions in sentences, and soon it will become natural.
