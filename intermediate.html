📅 July 11, 2026 [0 days, 0 hours old] - US Seeks Iran's Pledge on Shipping Safety in Strait of Hormuz
The United States is pushing Iran to formally promise to stop military actions against ships in the Strait of Hormuz, with high-level negotiations set to restart in Oman.

Intermediate ESL: International Diplomacy and Maritime Security

I. The News Brief

The United States is pressing Iran to pledge an end to any hostile actions against vessels in the vital Strait of Hormuz. This critical demand follows recent incidents that have raised global concerns about maritime safety. High-level US officials are preparing for important diplomatic meetings this weekend. Vice-President JD Vance is among US officials expected to take part in negotiations resuming on Saturday in Oman. These negotiations aim to secure a formal commitment from Iran. The US wants Iran to pledge to stop shooting at ships in the important shipping lane. International observers are closely monitoring these diplomatic efforts to ensure regional stability. The discussions will focus on establishing clear protocols for preventing future conflicts. It is hoped that a lasting agreement can be reached to protect international shipping. The Strait of Hormuz is a strategically important waterway for global oil shipments.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. pledge (verb): To formally promise or commit to do something, often in a serious or official way.
2. Strait of Hormuz (noun phrase): A narrow sea passage connecting the Persian Gulf with the Gulf of Oman and the Arabian Sea, crucial for global oil transport.
3. officials (noun): People who hold a position of authority in an organization or government, responsible for specific duties.
4. negotiations (noun): Formal discussions between people trying to reach an agreement or resolve a dispute.
5. resuming (verb (present participle)): Starting again after a pause or interruption; continuing from where something left off.

Grammar Focus: Passive Voice
The passive voice is used when the action is more important than the agent (the person or thing doing the action), or when the agent is unknown or not important. In this construction, the subject receives the action. Here, 'expected to take part' emphasizes the state of being expected rather than who is doing the expecting.
Example from the text: "Vice-President JD Vance is among US officials expected to take part in negotiations resuming on Saturday in Oman."

Intermediate ESL: Natural Disasters and Public Safety: Wildfire Incident in Spain

I. The News Brief

A large wildfire recently broke out near Los Gallardos in Almería, a region in southern Spain. This tragic incident has led to a significant loss of life and a high number of missing persons. Authorities have confirmed that at least twelve people are dead as a direct consequence of the fire. Emergency services are working tirelessly to locate the missing and to manage the ongoing crisis. Investigations will likely determine the exact cause of the fire and assess the full extent of the damage. The community is deeply affected by the tragic events, hoping for news about the missing individuals. II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

2. tragic incident (noun phrase): A very sad, unfortunate, or terrible event that causes suffering or distress.
3. perished (verb): Died, especially in a sudden, violent, or unexpected way, often referring to a disaster.
4. underscores (verb): Emphasizes or highlights the importance or truth of something, drawing attention to it.
5. ongoing crisis (noun phrase): A serious and difficult situation or period of danger that is currently happening and continuing.

The passive voice is used when the focus is on the action or the receiver of the action, rather than the performer of the action. It's often used when the agent is unknown, unimportant, or obvious, or when describing unfortunate events. We form it with a form of 'to be' followed by a past participle verb.
Example from the text: "specifically twenty-three individuals, have been reported missing since the blaze began."

President Trump announced the end of a ceasefire with Iran following mutual military strikes, stating that US negotiators are still open to discussions with Iranian officials.

Intermediate ESL: International Relations and Geopolitics

I. The News Brief

US President Donald Trump recently declared that the ceasefire between the United States and Iran is now "over". This declaration came shortly after both nations engaged in trade strikes, exchanging military actions against each other. The President made his comments while speaking ahead of an important Nato summit. During his remarks, Trump emphasized that American negotiators remain prepared to continue discussions with Iran. He added that talks can proceed "if they want," indicating an open invitation for dialogue. The situation highlights ongoing tensions in the region, despite previous efforts to reduce conflict. High-ranking officials from both sides are closely monitoring developments. International observers are concerned about the potential for further escalation after these military exchanges. II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. ceasefire (noun): A temporary stop in fighting agreed by all sides involved in a war or conflict.
2. trade strikes (verb phrase): To exchange attacks or military actions with an opponent, usually meaning both sides have launched attacks.

4. summit (noun): An official meeting or conference between leaders, especially of governments or countries, to discuss important issues.

Reporting verbs are used to describe what someone said or thought. They are often followed by 'that' and then a clause (subject + verb). Using different reporting verbs (like 'declare', 'emphasize', 'add') can give more specific information about the speaker's intention or tone, rather than just using 'said'.

Ukrainian President Zelensky plans to urge NATO for more air defence systems, including interceptor missiles, during a meeting in Turkey following recent intense Russian attacks.

Intermediate ESL: International Military Aid and Ukraine's Defence Needs

I. The News Brief

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky is preparing to press NATO leaders for increased military assistance. His main focus will be securing additional air defence systems for his country. This urgent request comes after a period of intense Russian strikes across Ukraine. These attacks have highlighted Ukraine's critical need for better protection against aerial threats. Zelensky specifically aims to receive more interceptor missiles to counter incoming projectiles. He plans to argue his case strongly during an upcoming NATO meeting held in Turkey. The President believes these systems are vital for safeguarding Ukrainian cities and infrastructure. The ongoing conflict continues to demonstrate the importance of robust aerial protection. International support in this area is crucial for Ukraine's ability to defend itself effectively. Therefore, the outcome of this meeting could significantly impact the course of the war.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

2. air defence systems (noun phrase): Military equipment and strategies used to protect against attacks from aircraft or missiles, often designed to detect and destroy threats.

4. interceptor missiles (noun phrase): Guided missiles specifically designed to detect and destroy incoming enemy missiles or aircraft while they are in flight.

Reduced relative clauses help make sentences more concise by removing the relative pronoun (like 'which', 'who', 'that') and the verb 'to be' when they are followed by a participle (present or past). They provide essential or non-essential information about a noun.

Ukraine has issued a serious warning about a potential interceptor missile shortage following a significant Russian attack on Kyiv that involved numerous missiles and drones.

Intermediate ESL: Ukraine-Russia conflict and military defense

I. The News Brief

Ukraine has issued a serious warning about a potential interceptor missile shortage following a large-scale attack. On Sunday, the capital city of Kyiv experienced what President Zelensky described as a "massive Russian attack." This onslaught involved a significant number of aerial weapons, specifically 68 missiles and 351 strike drones. The Ukrainian President's statement emphasized the sheer scale and intensity of the recent aggression. Such widespread attacks place immense strain on Ukraine's air defense capabilities and existing stockpiles. The immediate concern is the depletion of crucial defensive munitions needed to counter future threats. Ukrainian officials are urgently trying to address the declining reserves to maintain effective protection for their cities. The warning highlights the critical need for continued international support to supply more air defense systems. Nineteen people were reportedly killed in the Kyiv region as a direct result of this attack. This incident underscores the ongoing challenges Ukraine faces in protecting its civilian population and infrastructure.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. interceptor missile shortage (noun phrase): A situation where a country does not have enough missiles designed to stop enemy attacks in the air, creating a lack of vital defensive weapons.
2. massive (adjective): Something extremely large in size, amount, or degree; very big or powerful.
3. strike drones (noun phrase): Unmanned aerial vehicles that are designed to attack targets, often equipped with bombs or missiles.

The tense in the reported clause often shifts backwards.
Example from the text: "President Zelensky described as a "massive Russian attack.""

Intermediate ESL: Middle East Politics and Geopolitics

I. The News Brief

Senior Iranian officials recently gathered for the funeral of an influential ayatollah, drawing international attention. The ongoing conflict has significantly impacted the political landscape and regional stability. It is often used in news to report events where the agent is unknown, unimportant, or the focus is on the result.
Example from the text: "Mojtaba Khamenei, who is a prominent figure in Iran, has not been seen publicly for some time."
