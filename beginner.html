📅 June 10, 2026 [0 days, 0 hours old] - US and Iran Trade New Threats
The United States and Iran are exchanging new warnings.

Beginner ESL: Tensions between the United States and Iran

I. The News Brief

President Trump is the leader of the United States. Iran is a country in the Middle East. There were some attacks between the two sides. Now, Trump and Iran are trading threats. President Trump warns Iran. He says Iran "will have to pay the price". This is because Iran takes too long to agree a deal. Tehran is the capital city of Iran. Tehran vows retaliation. They will fight back if there are new attacks.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. trading threats (verb phrase): People or countries say bad things they might do to each other.
2. warns (verb): To tell someone about a possible danger or problem.
3. pay the price (idiom): To suffer bad results because of something you did.
4. agree a deal (verb phrase): To decide together to make an official arrangement or agreement.
5. vows retaliation (verb phrase): To promise to hurt someone back because they hurt you.

Grammar Focus: Using "Because"
We use "because" to show the reason for something. It answers the question "Why?". You say something happened, then "because", then the reason.
Example from the text: "This is because Iran takes too long to agree a deal."

📅 June 09, 2026 [1 day, 0 hours old] - Trump Discusses Netanyahu and Iran with BBC
US President Trump spoke with the BBC about the Israeli leader and the situation in Iran.

Beginner ESL: International Relations and Political News

I. The News Brief

Donald Trump is the US President. He spoke to the BBC reporter Sarah Smith. She asked him many questions. President Trump talked about Israeli leader Netanyahu. He said Netanyahu did not defy him. To defy means to refuse to obey. The reporter also asked about the war in Iran. Trump shared his thoughts on the Middle East. This conversation was important for world news. People want to know about these leaders' relationship.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. President (noun): The highest political leader of a country.
2. reporter (noun): A person who finds and shares news stories with the public.
3. asked (verb): To put a question to someone to get information or an answer.
4. defy (verb): To refuse to obey someone or something, often in an open way.
5. relationship (noun): The way two people or countries are connected or behave with each other.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense (Negative)
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that finished in the past. To make a negative sentence in the simple past, we use 'did not' + the base form of the verb. For example, 'He did not go.' It shows something did not happen.
Example from the text: "He said Netanyahu did not defy him."

Beginner ESL: Middle East Conflict: Ceasefire Talks

I. The News Brief

Grammar Focus: Simple Present Tense
We use the Simple Present Tense for facts, habits, or scheduled events. It describes actions that are generally true or happen regularly. We often use it when we talk about agreements or conditions too.
Example from the text: "Israel and Lebanon agree on a plan."
