📅 May 09, 2026 - Understanding Today's News: Putin's Speech
This lesson helps you understand news about a speech by President Putin and important vocabulary.

Beginner ESL: Putin's Victory Day Speech

I. The News Brief

It was Victory Day in Russia on May 9. President Putin gave a speech. He spoke about his country's special military operation in Ukraine. He wanted to justify this operation to the people. Putin talked a lot about Nato. He said Nato was a big threat to Russia. He used his annual speech to explain his ideas. The speech happened at a parade. This year, the parade was scaled-back. Putin criticized Nato in his speech.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. special military operation (noun phrase): Russia's name for its war in Ukraine.
2. justify (verb): To give a good reason for an action.
3. Nato (proper noun): A group of countries that work to protect each other.
4. threat (noun): Something that can cause danger.
5. scaled-back (adjective): Smaller or less important than usual.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that happened and finished in the past. This news brief talks about past events. For example, 'President Putin gave a speech.' The action 'give' happened and finished. Many verbs in the news brief are in the simple past.
Example from the text: "President Putin gave a speech."

Beginner ESL: News about a Leader's Speech

I. The News Brief

Russia celebrated Victory Day with a parade. The parade was smaller than usual this year. President Putin gave his annual speech. He spoke about NATO. He also talked about the special military operation in Ukraine. He tried to justify this operation to the people.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

3. annual (adjective): Happening once every year.
4. justify (verb): To give a good reason for something.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that happened and finished in the past. Many verbs end with -ed (like "celebrated" or "talked"). Some verbs change their form completely (like "gave" from "give" or "spoke" from "speak").
Example from the text: "President Putin gave his annual speech."

Beginner ESL: Understanding News

I. The News Brief

Today, a leader named Putin gave a speech. He spoke at a Victory Day parade. The parade was smaller this year. Putin talked about Nato. He said Nato was bad. He also gave reasons for his country's actions in Ukraine.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. denounces (verb): says strongly that something is bad.
2. scaled-back (adjective): made smaller or less than usual.
3. justify (verb): to give a good reason for something.
4. operation (noun): a planned action or activity.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that finished in the past. For regular verbs, we often add '-ed'. For irregular verbs, the form changes. In the news, it says: "The Russian leader used his annual speech..." The verb 'use' becomes 'used' because the action happened in the past.

Beginner ESL: News About Russia

I. The News Brief

It was a special day in Russia called Victory Day. Mr. Putin is the leader of Russia. He gave a speech at a parade. The parade was smaller than usual. Mr. Putin talked about the conflict in Ukraine. He said Russia's actions in Ukraine were necessary.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. Leader (noun): The person who guides a country or group.
2. Speech (noun): When someone talks to many people.
3. Parade (noun): Many people walk or cars drive in a line for a special day.
4. Smaller than usual (phrase): Less big than it normally is.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that finished before now. It tells us about something that already happened.
Example from the text: "He gave a speech at a parade." ('gave' is the simple past of 'give').
