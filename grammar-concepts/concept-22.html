Grammar Concepts #22

Measurements & Shapes

  • Key Points
  • Practical Examples in Context
  • Key Points to Remember

Core Idea

Understanding the different ways to describe measurements and shapes is crucial for non-native English speakers. This image provides a clear distinction between noun and adjective forms of common shapes and measurements. Let’s break down these concepts with detailed explanations and examples.

Key Points

Introduction

Shapes

1. Circle / Circular:

  • Noun: It is a circle.
  • Adjective: It is circular.

Example Sentences:

  • The children sat in a circle.
  • The garden has a circular layout.

2. Square / Square:

  • Noun: It is a square.
  • Adjective: It is square.

Example Sentences:

  • We drew a square on the board.
  • The room is perfectly square.

3. Triangle / Triangular:

  • Noun: It is a triangle.
  • Adjective: It is triangular.

Example Sentences:

  • She cut the sandwich into a triangle.
  • The triangular sign warns drivers of a sharp turn ahead.

4. Oval / Oval:

  • Noun: It is an oval.
  • Adjective: It is oval.

Example Sentences:

  • The artist painted an oval on the canvas.
  • The mirror is oval-shaped.

5. Rectangle / Rectangular:

  • Noun: It is a rectangle.
  • Adjective: It is rectangular.

Example Sentences:

  • The table is shaped like a rectangle.
  • They bought a rectangular rug for the living room.

Measurements

For many measurements, there is a noun style and an adjective style.

Noun Style Examples:

  • What is its size?
  • The size of the box is large.
  • What is its length?
  • The length of the table is six feet.
  • What is its width?
  • The width of the road is narrow.
  • What is its depth?
  • The depth of the pool is ten feet.
  • What is its height?
  • The height of the building is impressive.
  • What is its weight?
  • The weight of the package is heavy.
  • What is its age?
  • The age of the tree is old.
  • What is its temperature?
  • The temperature of the soup is hot.
  • What is its speed?
  • The speed of the car is fast.
  • What is the distance?
  • The distance between the two towns is far.
  • What is the price?
  • The price of the dress is expensive.
  • What is its value?
  • The value of the painting is high.

Adjective Style Examples:

  • How large/small is it?
  • The box is large.
  • How long/short is it?
  • The table is six feet long.
  • How wide/narrow is it?
  • The road is narrow.
  • How deep/shallow is it?
  • The pool is ten feet deep.
  • How high/short is it?
  • The building is high.
  • How heavy/light is it?
  • The package is heavy.
  • How old/new is it?
  • The tree is old.
  • How hot/cold is it?
  • The soup is hot.
  • How fast/slow is it?
  • The car is fast.
  • How far/near is it?
  • The towns are far apart.
  • How expensive/cheap is it?
  • The dress is expensive.
  • How valuable is it?
  • The painting is highly valuable.

Practical Examples in Context

To further clarify, let’s use these measurements and shapes in different contexts:

1. Shopping:

  • Noun Style: What is the price of this shirt? The price is reasonable.
  • Adjective Style: How expensive is this shirt? It is quite cheap.

2. Describing Objects:

  • Noun Style: What is the size of your new apartment? The size is huge.
  • Adjective Style: How large is your new apartment? It is very spacious.

3. Giving Directions:

  • Noun Style: What is the distance to the nearest gas station? The distance is short.
  • Adjective Style: How far is the nearest gas station? It is very close.

Key Points to Remember

  • Shapes:
  • Use the noun form to identify the shape.
  • Use the adjective form to describe the shape.
  • Measurements:
  • The noun form asks for the measurement.
  • The adjective form describes the measurement.
  • Context Usage:
  • Context determines whether you should use the noun or adjective form.
  • Both forms are essential for clear and precise communication.

Understanding and using these grammar concepts correctly will enhance your English communication skills, especially when describing shapes and measurements in various contexts.
