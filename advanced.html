Advanced Grammar: Use of 'following' as a sophisticated preposition for cause/effect

It provides a concise and formal way to connect actions with their preceding circumstances, common in news reporting and academic writing.
Example from the text: "Israel has escalated its military operations in Lebanon following increased cross-border aggression."

When used to show a result or consequence, they typically follow the main clause and comment on its outcome.
