📅 July 21, 2026 [0 days, 0 hours old] - US Imposes 50% Tariffs on Canada, Escalating Trade Tensions
This lesson explains how recent US tariffs on Canada have significantly increased trade disagreements between the two North American neighbours, prompting Canada to intensify talks.

Intermediate ESL: International Trade Disputes and Economic Relations between the US and Canada

I. The News Brief

The United States, under President Trump, recently imposed significant 50% tariffs on goods imported from Canada. These new duties represent a substantial increase in taxes on specific Canadian products entering the US market. The decision by the US government has led to a major escalation in existing trade disagreements between the two nations. This move highlights the strained economic relationship currently affecting the North American neighbours. In response, Canada's representative, Carney, vows to actively work towards resolving these issues. He stated his intention to intensify ongoing trade talks with American officials. Such protectionist measures often aim to protect domestic industries but can provoke retaliatory actions. Economists are closely watching the situation, concerned about the potential negative impact on both economies. The increased trade tensions could disrupt supply chains and raise prices for consumers in both countries. Both sides hope to reach a resolution quickly to avoid further economic damage.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. tariffs (noun): Taxes or duties paid on a particular class of imports or exports.
2. escalation (noun): An increase in the intensity or seriousness of something, such as a conflict or a problem.
3. vows (verb): To make a solemn promise or commitment to do something, often with strong determination.
4. intensify (verb): To become or make something more intense, stronger, or more extreme.
5. trade tensions (noun phrase): Strained relations between countries that result from disagreements over trade policies, such as tariffs or quotas.

Grammar Focus: Present Perfect Tense
The Present Perfect tense is used to describe actions or states that began in the past and continue into the present, or have an effect on the present. It often connects a past event to its current relevance without specifying a precise past time. It's formed with 'has/have' + past participle.
Example from the text: "The decision by the US government has led to a major escalation in existing trade disagreements between the two nations."

📅 July 20, 2026 [0 days, 23 hours old] - US Soldier Killed in Iraq Following Iranian Attack

Intermediate ESL: Military conflict in the Middle East and US casualties

I. The News Brief

A US soldier was killed and another was injured following an Iranian attack in Iraq, according to recent reports. This specific incident has increased the tensions already present in the Middle East. The US military officially confirmed the casualty, which is a sad loss of life for the nation. Crucially, this event happened only a day after two other American soldiers lost their lives in a separate attack in Jordan. These tragic incidents underscore the dangerous conditions faced by military personnel deployed to conflict zones. Authorities are currently conducting detailed investigations into the precise nature of these assaults. The attack in Iraq reportedly involved advanced weaponry, indicating a deliberate and well-planned operation. Many observers believe these violent actions could significantly complicate ongoing diplomatic efforts. The Pentagon has indicated that it is evaluating various response options to protect its forces. Such repeated attacks pose a serious threat to regional stability and could lead to wider conflict as retaliation is considered.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. tensions (noun): A situation in which countries or groups feel angry or hostile towards each other, often without open fighting.
2. casualty (noun): A person injured or killed in an accident, war, or other violent event.
3. complicate (verb): To make something more difficult or complex to deal with, solve, or understand.
4. conflict (noun): A serious disagreement or argument, often leading to fighting between groups or nations.
5. retaliation (noun): The act of hurting someone or doing something bad to them because they have hurt you or done something bad to you; an act of revenge.

Grammar Focus: Passive Voice
The passive voice is used when the focus is on the action itself and the object of the action, rather than on who or what is performing the action. It is often used in news reports when the 'doer' is unknown, unimportant, or when the action is more significant. The structure is usually 'be' verb + past participle.
Example from the text: "A US soldier was killed and another was injured following an Iranian attack in Iraq, according to recent reports."

📅 July 19, 2026 [2 days, 0 hours old] - US and Iran Exchange Strikes Following Jordan Attack
Following the tragic deaths of two US soldiers in Jordan, the United States and Iran have engaged in a series of retaliatory military strikes, increasing regional instability.

Intermediate ESL: Geopolitical Tensions and Military Retaliation in the Middle East

I. The News Brief

The United States and Iran have recently been involved in a dangerous exchange of strikes, highlighting growing tensions in the Middle East. This escalation follows an incident where two US soldiers were tragically killed in Jordan, which prompted a strong reaction. In response, US forces have been actively targeting Iranian sites for eight consecutive nights, demonstrating a clear pattern of retaliation. These actions illustrate a clear and forceful response from the American side to the initial attack. Meanwhile, Iran stated that it fired drones towards the US military presence in Kuwait, adding another layer to the conflict. The ongoing situation raises serious concerns about broader regional conflict and overall stability in the area. Such an exchange of military actions carries significant risks for all parties involved and could worsen the existing crisis. International observers are closely monitoring the movements of both US forces and Iranian assets, hoping for a rapid de-escalation. The involvement of unmanned aircraft like drones adds a modern and complex dimension to these ongoing aerial operations. Both nations need to consider diplomatic solutions quickly to prevent further loss of life and safely de-escalate the conflict.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. exchange of strikes (noun phrase): A situation where two sides attack each other in response to previous attacks, often in a back-and-forth manner.
2. killed (verb (past participle)): To cause someone or something to die.
3. targeting (verb): To aim at a specific place or thing, often with the intention to attack or hit it.
4. forces (noun): A group of people, such as soldiers or police, who are organized for a particular purpose, especially fighting.
5. drones (noun): Unmanned aircraft, controlled remotely, often used for surveillance, reconnaissance, or military attacks.

Grammar Focus: The Passive Voice
The passive voice is a grammatical construction where the subject of a sentence receives the action rather than performing it. It's often used when the action's recipient is more important than the performer, or when the performer is unknown or obvious. It is formed using a form of 'to be' (e.g., is, am, are, was, were) followed by the past participle of the main verb.
Example from the text: "This escalation follows an incident where two US soldiers were tragically killed in Jordan, which prompted a strong reaction."

📅 July 18, 2026 [3 days, 0 hours old] - Middle East Tensions Escalate: US Strikes and Iranian Retaliation
Tensions in the Middle East have risen sharply after the United States carried out air strikes for seven consecutive nights, prompting Iran to retaliate against US allies in the region, including a power and water plant in Kuwait.

Intermediate ESL: International Conflict and Regional Security in the Middle East

I. The News Brief

Recent developments indicate a significant escalation of military activity in the Middle East. The United States has conducted targeted air strikes for seven consecutive nights in the region. These ongoing strikes are part of a broader response to previous incidents involving regional actors. In reaction to the US actions, Iran has retaliated against several US allies in the area. Kuwait, a key US ally, has reported that one of its critical infrastructure sites was attacked. Specifically, a power and water plant in Kuwait had been hit during these retaliatory actions. This incident marks a concerning shift towards more direct engagement in the simmering conflict. The repeated attacks are fueling fears about the overall stability and security of the Middle East. International bodies are closely observing the situation, urging all parties to exercise restraint. Further escalation could have severe implications for global energy markets and regional populations.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. strikes (noun): Military attacks, usually from the air, aimed at specific targets or locations.
2. consecutive (adjective): Following one after another without interruption; happening in a row.
3. retaliated (verb): Taken action in return for an attack or injury; responded with a similar aggressive act.
4. allies (noun): Countries, organizations, or people that have formally agreed to cooperate and support each other, especially in war or conflict.
5. plant (noun): A large industrial facility or factory where goods are made or energy/services are produced, such as a power plant or a water treatment plant.

Grammar Focus: The Passive Voice (Past Perfect)
The passive voice is used when the focus is on the action and the object receiving it, rather than the performer of the action. The past perfect passive is formed with 'had been' + the past participle of the main verb, and it indicates an action completed before a specific point in the past. It often highlights the result or impact of an action without necessarily naming who did it.
Example from the text: "Specifically, a power and water plant in Kuwait had been hit during these retaliatory actions."

Intermediate ESL: International relations, military actions, and escalating tensions between the US and Iran.

The United States recently launched new military strikes in the region, intensifying existing tensions. These actions follow previous warnings from the US regarding regional stability and maritime security. 📅 July 15, 2026 [5 days, 20 hours old] - US-Iran Tensions Escalate: Threats Over Trade Routes and Potential Strikes
Tensions between the US and Iran are escalating, with Iran threatening to block crucial trade routes and the US vowing strikes if diplomatic talks do not resume.

Intermediate ESL: International Relations and Geopolitics: US-Iran Conflict

I. The News Brief

Recent events highlight a significant escalation in the ongoing conflict between the United States and Iran. Iran has threatened to block crucial international trade routes, specifically mentioning the Strait of Hormuz. This action would severely disrupt global oil shipments and have widespread economic implications. In response, US President Donald Trump has vowed to strike key Iranian infrastructure, including bridges and power plants. The President's warning specified that US forces would launch fresh strikes if Iran does not agree to new diplomatic talks. Such military actions carry the risk of further destabilizing the already tense Middle East region. International observers are closely watching the situation, urging both nations to return to talks and de-escalate. The ultimate goal for many global leaders is to find a peaceful resolution to the dispute. However, both sides appear firm in their positions, making immediate reconciliation challenging. The global community remains concerned about the potential for wider conflict and its impact on stability.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. threatened to block (verb phrase): To state an intention to stop something, often by preventing access or movement, like closing a passage.
2. trade routes (noun phrase): Established paths or channels used for moving goods and merchandise between different regions or countries for commercial purposes.
3. vowed to strike (verb phrase): To make a serious and determined promise or solemn declaration to attack or initiate a military action against someone or something.
4. fresh strikes (noun phrase): New or recent military attacks, often implying a continuation or renewal of previous actions or a completely new offensive.
5. return to talks (verb phrase): To resume or restart diplomatic negotiations or discussions after a period of suspension, disagreement, or breakdown in communication.

Grammar Focus: Conditional Sentences (Type 1)
Type 1 conditional sentences describe real and possible situations in the future. They are formed with 'if' + simple present in the condition clause, and 'will' + base verb in the result clause. They express what will happen if a certain condition is met, highlighting a direct cause and effect relationship for future events.
Example from the text: "The President's warning specified that US forces would launch fresh strikes if Iran does not agree to new diplomatic talks."
