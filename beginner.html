📅 May 21, 2026 [0 days, 0 hours old] - US Charges Against Raúl Castro
The US government made charges against Raúl Castro about old events.

Beginner ESL: US and Cuba Relations

I. The News Brief

The US government made charges. These charges are against Raúl Castro of Cuba. Raúl Castro was an important leader. The charges are about an event in 1996. In 1996, two planes went down. The US says Castro was involved in murder. The charges include "conspiracy to kill" US people. They also include "destruction of aircraft". Cuba's president calls these charges a "political manoeuvre". He says the US has a special reason for them.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. charges (noun): official statements saying someone did something wrong.
2. leader (noun): a person who guides or controls a group or country.
3. event (noun): something important that happens.
4. involved (adjective): connected to something or part of an activity.
5. political manoeuvre (noun): a clever plan by a government to get an advantage.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that finished in the past. We often use words like "in 1996" or "yesterday." For regular verbs, we add "-ed." For irregular verbs, the form changes.
Example from the text: "The US government made charges."

📅 May 20, 2026 [1 day, 0 hours old] - Xi Jinping Meets World Leaders
President Xi Jinping recently met two important world leaders.

Beginner ESL: World Leaders and Important Diplomatic Meetings

I. The News Brief

Chinese President Xi Jinping hosts important leaders. He recently met two very important guests. First, he met President Donald Trump. Then, President Vladimir Putin came to visit. President Xi met Putin a few days after Trump. This event puts China in the spotlight. Xi Jinping wants to see China as a big global player. He wants China to talk to all nations. He does not want China to be tied to just one country. These presidential visits show China's important role in the world.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. hosts (verb): To welcome guests to your country or home.
2. spotlight (noun): Public attention; when everyone looks at someone or something.
3. days after (phrase): A few days later; not immediately following an event.
4. wants to see (phrase): Wishes or hopes for something to happen or for a situation to be true.
5. tied to (phrase): Strongly connected to, loyal to, or supporting one thing or group.

Grammar Focus: Simple Present Tense
We use the Simple Present Tense for facts, habits, and things that are generally true. We also use it to talk about wishes or desires that are always true or current.
Example from the text: "Xi Jinping wants to see China as a big global player."

📅 May 19, 2026 [2 days, 0 hours old] - Ebola Outbreak: Fear Spreads in Central Africa
People are afraid because a dangerous disease called Ebola is spreading quickly in central Africa.

Beginner ESL: Ebola outbreak and fear in central Africa

I. The News Brief

Ebola is a very serious sickness. Many people in central Africa have this disease. Doctors think hundreds of people are sick. This outbreak of Ebola is dangerous. Experts fear the real number of sick people is much higher. The disease is spreading very quickly. People say Ebola has tortured them. They are very worried now. This problem is happening in central Africa. Doctors and nurses work hard to help people.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. outbreak (noun): A sudden start of a disease that affects many people.
2. fear (verb): To be scared or worried that something bad will happen.
3. higher (adjective): A larger amount or number.
4. spreading (verb (present participle)): Moving and affecting more places or people.
5. tortured (verb (past participle)): Caused a lot of pain and suffering.

Grammar Focus: Present Continuous
We use the present continuous to talk about actions happening now or around now. We make it with 'be' (am, is, are) + verb-ing.
Example from the text: "The disease is spreading very quickly."

📅 May 17, 2026 [4 days, 1 hour old] - Ebola Outbreak Declared Global Health Emergency
The World Health Organization says the Ebola outbreak in DR Congo is now a global health emergency.

Beginner ESL: Public Health and Disease Outbreaks

I. The News Brief

The World Health Organization (WHO) helps people stay healthy. They made an important declaration about Ebola. Ebola is a very serious illness that makes people sick. There is an outbreak of Ebola in DR Congo. An outbreak means many people get sick in one area. The WHO now says this is a global health emergency. This means the problem needs help from many countries. About 246 people have caught the illness in DR Congo. Sadly, around 80 people have died because of this sickness. The WHO did not call it a pandemic emergency.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. declaration (noun): an official and important statement
2. outbreak (noun): when a disease suddenly begins to happen to many people at the same time
3. global health emergency (phrase): a very serious health problem that needs urgent action and help from countries all over the world
4. sickness (noun): the state of being ill; a disease or illness
5. pandemic emergency (phrase): a very serious disease that spreads quickly across many countries or continents, causing a widespread health crisis

Grammar Focus: There is / There are
We use 'There is' when we talk about one thing existing. We use 'There are' when we talk about more than one thing existing. It introduces what is present.
Example from the text: "There is an outbreak of Ebola in DR Congo."

📅 May 15, 2026 [6 days, 0 hours old] - Trump and Xi Conclude Talks
Leaders from the US and China met, but they did not make new trade deals.

Beginner ESL: International Relations and Trade

I. The News Brief

President Trump of the US met President Xi of China. They had important talks. The leaders met for two days. They tried to make new deals. The presidents wanted to improve trade. At the end, they did not conclude any new agreements. There were many ceremonies during the visit. These events were carefully planned. The leaders called the meeting 'very successful'. There were plenty of choreographed ceremonies but no trade breakthroughs.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

4. successful (adjective): When something ends well and achieves its goal.
5. ceremonies (noun): Formal public events with special customs and actions.

Grammar Focus: Using 'But' for Contrast
'But' is a word we use to show a difference or a contrast between two ideas. It connects two parts of a sentence when the second part says something different from the first part.
Example from the text: "There were plenty of choreographed ceremonies but no trade breakthroughs."
