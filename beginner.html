📅 July 02, 2026 [0 days, 0 hours old] - Kyiv Mourns After Major Russian Attack
Kyiv experienced a large attack and now mourns the people who died.

Beginner ESL: Russian attack on Kyiv

I. The News Brief

Kyiv is the capital city of Ukraine. Russia launched a very big attack on Kyiv. This was the most massive attack on the city. Many drones and missiles were used. The attack happened early in the morning. At least 18 people died. The mayor of Kyiv declared a day of mourning. People feel very sad because of the deaths. The capital city is now very quiet. Kyiv will remember this major event.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. most massive (adjective phrase): This means very, very big or the biggest.
2. attack (noun): When someone tries to hurt people or damage a place.
3. day of mourning (noun phrase): A special day when people are very sad for people who died.
4. capital (noun): The most important city in a country, where the government is.
5. major (adjective): This means very important or very big.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense
We use the simple past tense to talk about actions that happened and finished in the past. We often add '-ed' to regular verbs. Irregular verbs change their form.
Example from the text: "The mayor of Kyiv declared a day of mourning."

📅 June 26, 2026 [5 days, 23 hours old] - Earthquake Strikes Venezuela Amidst Political Uncertainty
A recent earthquake caused significant problems in Venezuela during a time when the country was already facing political difficulties.

Beginner ESL: Natural Disaster and Political Challenges in Venezuela

I. The News Brief

An earthquake hit Venezuela recently. It was a devastating blow for the country. People in Venezuela are now facing uncertainty. This happened when the country was already having problems. Two quakes, called "twin quakes," shook the land. These quakes came less than six months after another big event. Venezuela's then-leader, Nicolás Maduro, was seized. US forces were responsible for taking him. So, Venezuela is dealing with natural disaster and political issues together. The country faces a very unclear future because of these events.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. devastating blow (noun phrase): a very bad event that causes great damage or sadness for a country.
2. uncertainty (noun): a situation where you do not know what will happen next; a feeling of being unsure.
3. twin quakes (noun phrase): two earthquakes that happen very close together in time or location.
4. then-leader (noun): a person who was the main person in charge of a country at a specific time in the past.
5. seized (verb): taken by force or official power, often suddenly and without warning.

Grammar Focus: Simple Past Tense (Passive Voice)
We use the Simple Past tense to talk about actions that finished in the past. When the subject of the sentence receives the action, we use the passive voice. We form it with 'was' or 'were' and the past participle form of the verb. For example, 'was seized' means someone else performed the action of seizing.
Example from the text: "Venezuela's then-leader, Nicolás Maduro, was seized."
