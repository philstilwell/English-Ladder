📅 July 17, 2026 [0 days, 0 hours old] - US Launches New Strikes Amid Tensions with Iran
The United States has carried out fresh military strikes, while Iran claims these actions have damaged civilian infrastructure and the US has boarded a ship in a key waterway.

Intermediate ESL: International relations, military actions, and escalating tensions between the US and Iran.

I. The News Brief

The United States recently launched new military strikes in the region, intensifying existing tensions. These actions follow previous warnings from the US regarding regional stability and maritime security. Iran quickly responded, asserting that the American strikes specifically targeted civilian infrastructure. According to Tehran, bridges were among the structures hit, causing concern for the local population. Such incidents often escalate diplomatic disagreements into more serious confrontations. Separately, the US military confirmed that it had boarded a commercial vessel in the Strait of Hormuz. The Strait of Hormuz is a crucial maritime gateway for global oil shipments. This boarding operation occurred as part of ongoing efforts to prevent illegal activities in the international waters. Iran condemned both the strikes and the boarding, calling them violations of its sovereignty. The international community is closely watching these developments, hoping to de-escalate the volatile situation.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. strikes (noun): Military attacks, usually carried out from the air or with missiles.
2. civilian infrastructure (noun): Public facilities and services that support a society, such as roads, bridges, power grids, and water systems, used by ordinary people.
3. maritime gateway (noun): An important sea route or passage that ships use to enter or exit a region, often critical for trade.
4. international waters (noun): Areas of the sea that are not under the exclusive control or jurisdiction of any single country, open to all nations.
5. de-escalate (verb): To reduce the intensity, severity, or danger of a conflict, dispute, or tense situation.

Grammar Focus: The Passive Voice
The passive voice is used when the focus is on the action or the receiver of the action, rather than the doer of the action. It is formed with a form of the verb 'to be' and the past participle of the main verb. We often use it when the doer of the action is unknown, unimportant, or obvious from the context.
Example from the text: "According to Tehran, bridges were among the structures hit, causing concern for the local population."

I. The News Brief

Citizens in several Ukrainian cities have begun to stage protests. These demonstrations are a reaction to President Zelensky's recent removal of the defence minister. Mykhailo Fedorov's dismissal has sparked considerable public outrage. Many people are demanding a clear explanation for this unexpected decision. The president, however, has not yet offered any official reason for the change. This lack of transparency is causing great upset among various groups. Grammar Focus: Present Perfect Simple
The Present Perfect Simple is used to talk about actions or states that happened at an unspecified time in the past but have a connection to the present, or actions that started in the past and continue up to now. It often emphasizes the result of an action rather than when it happened.

Tensions between the US and Iran are escalating, with Iran threatening to block crucial trade routes and the US vowing strikes if diplomatic talks do not resume.

Intermediate ESL: International Relations and Geopolitics: US-Iran Conflict

I. The News Brief

Recent events highlight a significant escalation in the ongoing conflict between the United States and Iran. Iran has threatened to block crucial international trade routes, specifically mentioning the Strait of Hormuz. This action would severely disrupt global oil shipments and have widespread economic implications. In response, US President Donald Trump has vowed to strike key Iranian infrastructure, including bridges and power plants. The President's warning specified that US forces would launch fresh strikes if Iran does not agree to new diplomatic talks. Such military actions carry the risk of further destabilizing the already tense Middle East region. International observers are closely watching the situation, urging both nations to return to talks and de-escalate. The ultimate goal for many global leaders is to find a peaceful resolution to the dispute. However, both sides appear firm in their positions, making immediate reconciliation challenging. The global community remains concerned about the potential for wider conflict and its impact on stability.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. threatened to block (verb phrase): To state an intention to stop something, often by preventing access or movement, like closing a passage.
2. trade routes (noun phrase): Established paths or channels used for moving goods and merchandise between different regions or countries for commercial purposes.
3. vowed to strike (verb phrase): To make a serious and determined promise or solemn declaration to attack or initiate a military action against someone or something.
4. fresh strikes (noun phrase): New or recent military attacks, often implying a continuation or renewal of previous actions or a completely new offensive.
5. return to talks (verb phrase): To resume or restart diplomatic negotiations or discussions after a period of suspension, disagreement, or breakdown in communication.

Grammar Focus: Conditional Sentences (Type 1)
Type 1 conditional sentences describe real and possible situations in the future. They are formed with 'if' + simple present in the condition clause, and 'will' + base verb in the result clause. They express what will happen if a certain condition is met, highlighting a direct cause and effect relationship for future events.
Example from the text: "The President's warning specified that US forces would launch fresh strikes if Iran does not agree to new diplomatic talks."

Intermediate ESL: Emergency Response and Safety After a Bar Fire in Thailand

I. The News Brief

A severe fire engulfed a popular bar located in Bangkok's Chatuchak district, causing a tragic incident. The blaze resulted in a significant loss of life, with numerous people unable to escape the inferno. Reports indicate that many patrons were still inside the establishment when the fire broke out unexpectedly. Emergency services were promptly dispatched to the scene, responding to urgent calls for help. Upon their arrival, firefighters faced a chaotic situation as people desperately tried to flee the burning building. Eyewitnesses described seeing individuals running through flames to get outside and find safety. Tragically, at least 27 lives were lost, and 25 individuals were critically injured in the incident. Medical teams were immediately dispatched to provide urgent care at the scene. They were quickly transported to various hospitals where they are currently receiving treatment. Investigations are ongoing to determine the exact cause of this devastating fire and prevent future tragedies.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. engulfed (verb): To completely surround or cover something quickly and intensely.
2. patrons (noun): Customers or regular visitors of a particular establishment, such as a restaurant or bar.
3. dispatched (verb): Sent off to a destination or for a special purpose, especially quickly.
4. firefighters (noun): People whose job is to put out fires and rescue people from dangerous situations.
5. critically injured (adjective phrase): Very seriously hurt or wounded, in a life-threatening or extremely dangerous condition.

It is particularly useful when the agent (who did the action) is unknown, unimportant, or obvious from the context, or when we want to sound more formal.

📅 July 11, 2026 [6 days, 0 hours old] - US Seeks Iran's Pledge on Shipping Safety in Strait of Hormuz
The United States is pushing Iran to formally promise to stop military actions against ships in the Strait of Hormuz, with high-level negotiations set to restart in Oman.

Intermediate ESL: International Diplomacy and Maritime Security

I. The News Brief

The United States is pressing Iran to pledge an end to any hostile actions against vessels in the vital Strait of Hormuz. This critical demand follows recent incidents that have raised global concerns about maritime safety. High-level US officials are preparing for important diplomatic meetings this weekend. Vice-President JD Vance is among US officials expected to take part in negotiations resuming on Saturday in Oman. These negotiations aim to secure a formal commitment from Iran. The US wants Iran to pledge to stop shooting at ships in the important shipping lane. International observers are closely monitoring these diplomatic efforts to ensure regional stability. The discussions will focus on establishing clear protocols for preventing future conflicts. It is hoped that a lasting agreement can be reached to protect international shipping. The Strait of Hormuz is a strategically important waterway for global oil shipments.

II. Vocabulary & Grammar Focus

1. pledge (verb): To formally promise or commit to do something, often in a serious or official way.
2. Strait of Hormuz (noun phrase): A narrow sea passage connecting the Persian Gulf with the Gulf of Oman and the Arabian Sea, crucial for global oil transport.
3. officials (noun): People who hold a position of authority in an organization or government, responsible for specific duties.
4. negotiations (noun): Formal discussions between people trying to reach an agreement or resolve a dispute.
5. resuming (verb (present participle)): Starting again after a pause or interruption; continuing from where something left off.

Grammar Focus: Passive Voice
The passive voice is used when the action is more important than the agent (the person or thing doing the action), or when the agent is unknown or not important. In this construction, the subject receives the action. Here, 'expected to take part' emphasizes the state of being expected rather than who is doing the expecting.
Example from the text: "Vice-President JD Vance is among US officials expected to take part in negotiations resuming on Saturday in Oman."
