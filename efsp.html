Pharmaceutical English

The course is built for advanced ESL learners who work in clinical development, clinical operations, regulatory affairs, pharmacovigilance, medical affairs, quality, CMC, manufacturing, biostatistics, data management, market access, commercial compliance, or pharmaceutical-adjacent leadership roles. It teaches the vocabulary, sentence patterns, caveats, and professional judgment needed for development strategy, TPP alignment, IND readiness, NDA and BLA planning, protocol reviews, GCP conversations, endpoint and estimand discussions, safety triage, pharmacovigilance, CMC updates, quality events, labeling, promotional review, medical affairs boundaries, payer evidence, real-world evidence, biosimilars, lifecycle management, and launch governance.

Core topics

  • Drug development strategy, unmet need, target product profile, indication, mechanism of action, standard of care, evidence logic, and cross-functional decision criteria
  • Regulatory pathways, IND, NDA, BLA, accelerated approval, agency interactions, meeting packages, regulatory feedback, and sponsor risk
  • Clinical trial design, protocol language, GCP, informed consent, randomization, blinding, endpoints, estimands, intercurrent events, missing data, statistical interpretation, and clinical relevance
  • Pharmacovigilance, AE, SAE, SUSAR, MedDRA, case narratives, causality, signal detection, risk-benefit, label updates, REMS, FAERS, and postmarketing safety
  • CMC, CGMP, API, specifications, batch records, deviations, OOS, CAPA, process validation, stability, tech transfer, labeling, fair balance, off-label boundaries, MLR, medical affairs, HEOR, market access, RWD, RWE, biosimilars, and launch readiness

Materials included

  • Instructor guide with eight 90-minute module plans
  • Participant workbook with pharmaceutical-workplace practice pages
  • Dialogue lab with realistic TPP, IND readiness, endpoint, enrollment, safety signal, data readout, quality deviation, promotional claim, MSL, payer evidence, biosimilar, and launch-readiness conversations
  • Jargon quick reference for pharma terminology, evidence-safe meeting moves, and contrast pairs

Cultural Leadership in US Branches

The course is built for advanced Japanese and Chinese managers who need more than vocabulary. It gives learners a map of American workplace directness and repeated practice with the line between confident leadership and sufficient pushback.

Core topics

  • Reading American idea-combat without assuming hostility
  • Using evidence-based pushback without over-apologizing
  • Managing interruptions, public disagreement, and meeting closure
  • Giving direct feedback while preserving dignity
  • Translating between headquarters expectations and US branch norms

Materials included

  • Instructor guide with eight 90-minute module plans
  • Participant workbook with practice pages and reflection prompts
  • Scenario cards for role-play and coaching
  • Quick reference for in-the-moment pushback language
